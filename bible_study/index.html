üî¨ Study Methodology

1. Historical Context

Understanding the original commentator's time period, theological background, and intended audience.

Example: Matthew Henry's pastoral heart shaped his practical applications for 18th-century congregations.

2. Textual Analysis

Preserving original language while providing modern translations and explanations of archaic terms.

Example: Henry's "condescending love" means willingly lowering oneself to serve personally.

3. Contemporary Bridge

Connecting timeless principles to modern Christian living and contemporary challenges.

Example: Judas's "concern for the poor" mirrors modern criticism of generous worship spending.
