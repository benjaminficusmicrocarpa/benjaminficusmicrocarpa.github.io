📖 Henry's Structural Analysis

Henry's Original Framework:
"In these verses we have, I. The kind visit our Lord Jesus paid to his friends at Bethany (v. 1). II. The kind entertainment which his friends there gave him: They made him a supper (v. 2). III. The particular respect which Mary showed him, above the rest, in anointing his feet with sweet ointment (v. 3). IV. Judas's dislike of Mary's compliment... V. Christ's justification of what Mary did..."
🔍 What Henry is Doing:

Henry structures his commentary like a careful teacher, breaking down complex narratives into teachable moments. Notice how he sees each person's actions as deliberate spiritual lessons for us to learn from.

1️⃣ "As a preface to the passover he intended to celebrate"
📜 Henry's Exact Words:
"Devout men set time apart before, to prepare themselves for that solemnity, and thus it became our Lord Jesus to fulfil all righteousness. Thus he has set us an example of solemn self-sequestration, before the solemnities of the gospel passover; let us hear the voice crying, Prepare ye the way of the Lord."
🎯 Contemporary Application:

"Solemn self-sequestration" = Intentional spiritual preparation through withdrawal from normal activities

Modern Practice: Before major spiritual seasons (Advent, Lent, personal retreats), deliberately create space for reflection

Henry's Insight: Even Christ needed preparation time - how much more do we?

2️⃣ "As a voluntary exposing of himself to the fury of his enemies"
📜 Henry's Exact Words:
"Now that his hour was at hand he came within their reach, and freely offered himself to them, though he had shown them how easily he could evade all their snares... Our Lord Jesus was voluntary in his sufferings; his life was not forced from him, but resigned: Lo, I come."
🎯 The Paradox Henry Reveals:

"Voluntary exposing" = Choosing vulnerability when you have the power to stay safe

The Tension: Christ could "evade all snares" but chose not to

Modern Challenge: When is God calling us to strategic vulnerability rather than self-protection?

Henry's Principle: "As there is a time when we are allowed to shift for our own preservation, so there is a time when we are called to hazard our lives in the cause of God"

3️⃣ "As an instance of his kindness to his friends at Bethany"
📜 Henry's Exact Words:
"This was a farewell visit; he came to take leave of them, and to leave with them words of comfort against the day of trial that was approaching. Note, Though Christ depart for a time from his people, he will give them intimations that he departs in love, and not in anger."
🎯 Henry's Pastoral Insight:

"Farewell visit" = Intentional closure that prepares people for absence

"Words of comfort against the day of trial" = Prophetic preparation for difficult times ahead

Application: How do we "take leave" well when seasons change?

Henry sees Christ modeling how to end seasons well:

  • • Give clear intimations of your departure
  • • Ensure people know it's "in love, not anger"
  • • Provide "words of comfort" for the trials ahead
  • • Make the farewell itself a gift of preparation
📖 Henry's Threefold Character Analysis
"Now by this her love to Christ appears to have been, (1.) A generous love; so far from sparing necessary charges in his service, she is as ingenious to create an occasion of expense in religion as most are to avoid it... (2.) A condescending love; she not only bestowed her ointment upon Christ, but with her own hands poured it upon him... (3.) A believing love; there was faith working by this love, faith in Jesus as the Messiah, the Christ, the Anointed..."
1. Generous Love

Henry's phrase: "ingenious to create an occasion of expense"

Meaning: Cleverly finding ways to spend on God rather than avoid spending

Modern equivalent: Looking for opportunities to give sacrificially rather than excuses to hold back

Henry's Challenge:

Most people are "ingenious" to avoid religious expense. Mary was "ingenious" to create it. Which are you?

2. Condescending Love

Henry's insight: "she might have ordered one of her servants to have done"

18th century "condescending": Willingly lowering yourself to serve personally

The principle: True love doesn't delegate what it can do personally

Henry's Application:

"True love, as it does not spare charges, so it does not spare pains, in honouring Christ."

3. Believing Love

Henry's connection: Faith in Jesus as "the Christ, the Anointed"

The theology: She anoints the one who is THE anointed one

Symbolic meaning: Recognizing and responding to Christ's divine appointment

Henry's Conclusion:

"God's Anointed should be our Anointed. Has God poured on him the oil of gladness above his fellows? Let us pour on him the ointment of our best affections above all competitors."

🌸 "The House Was Filled With the Fragrance" - Henry's Symbolic Interpretation
📜 Henry's Exact Words:
"The filling of the house with the pleasant odour of the ointment may intimate to us, (1.) That those who entertain Christ in their hearts and houses bring a sweet odour into them; Christ's presence brings with it an ointment and perfume which rejoice the heart. (2.) Honours done to Christ are comforts to all his friends and followers; they are to God and good men an offering of a sweet-smelling savour."
🎯 Henry's Layered Teaching:

Layer 1: Literal - The physical fragrance filled the room

Layer 2: Personal - Christ's presence brings spiritual "fragrance" to our lives

Layer 3: Community - Worship that honors Christ encourages other believers

Layer 4: Cosmic - Such worship pleases God as "sweet-smelling savour"

Henry's Method: He sees physical details as windows into spiritual realities. The fragrance isn't just mentioned - it teaches us about the effect of true worship.

📖 Henry's Analysis: "Why was this waste?"
"Judas calls this a waste, which was really a worthy work. Note, It is not strange if that which is done for the honour of Christ, though ever so well done, be censured and found fault with by those that are enemies to him and to his kingdom. Covetous people are commonly hard upon those that are generous, and fasten the charge of foolish expense upon that charity which they themselves should rather be ashamed of their sordid parsimony."
📜 Henry's 18th Century Context:

"Sordid parsimony" = Disgraceful stinginess

"Fasten the charge" = Make accusations against

Henry's insight: The covetous always criticize the generous to justify their own stinginess

🎯 21st Century Application:

Modern "waste" criticisms:

  • • "That money could help the poor instead"
  • • "Why spend so much on church buildings?"
  • • "Shouldn't worship be simple and cheap?"
  • • "That's excessive religious spending"

Henry's warning: Check if your criticism comes from genuine concern or hidden covetousness.

🔍 Henry's Character Diagnosis: The Thief Revealed
"He said this, not that he cared for the poor (a charitable concern for them was a very good colour for his covetousness), but because he was a thief. Note, (1.) It is no new thing for bad men to cover bad designs with good pretences... (2.) Those who put the cheat upon others commonly put it first upon themselves, and with good words and fair speeches deceive their own hearts."
Henry's Two-Stage Deception Process:
Stage 1: Self-Deception

"Put the cheat upon themselves first" - They convince themselves their motives are pure

Modern example: "I'm not being stingy, I'm being a good steward"

Stage 2: Public Deception

"Cover bad designs with good pretences" - Then they deceive others using spiritual language

Modern example: Using "concern for the poor" to criticize generous worship

Henry's Warning: "Those who thus abuse the poor by making them serve their covetousness, and patronize it, do but add sin to sin."

🛡️ Henry's Analysis: How Christ Defends His People
"Our Lord Jesus puts a favourable construction upon this that Mary had done, and makes the best of it... Christ's advocates for his people are never at a loss for something to say in their defence, when they act from a principle of love to him, though they may be mistaken in the manner of expressing it."
📜 Henry's Exact Observation:
"Let her alone; why trouble ye her? She has done what she could, and what she did was seasonable... She has kept this against the day of my burying. Providence so ordered it that this should be done now, because there would not be time to do it when he was dead."
🎯 Henry's Theological Principle:

"Favourable construction" = Christ interprets our imperfect service in the best possible light

The Divine Pattern: When love motivates action, Christ defends even imperfect expressions

Modern comfort: Christ advocates for sincere believers even when their methods are questioned

📖 Henry's Scripture Connections

Henry connects this passage to multiple scriptures:

Romans 8:34 - Christ as our Advocate

"Who is he that condemneth? It is Christ that died, yea rather, that is risen again, who is even at the right hand of God, who also maketh intercession for us."

1 John 2:1 - Our Advocate with the Father

"If any man sin, we have an advocate with the Father, Jesus Christ the righteous."

Mark 14:8 - "She has done what she could"

Henry notes this is Christ's standard: not perfection, but sincere effort within our capacity.

🎯 Henry's Complete Practical Framework

📖 Henry's Summary Challenge:
"Let us learn hence, (1.) To be generous in our expenses for the honour of Christ... (2.) To be ourselves personally employed in serving Christ... (3.) To be forward to own Christ, and show him respect, when others are shy of him... (4.) Not to condemn as waste that which is laid out in the service of Christ and for his honour."
Henry's 18th Century Applications:
  • • "Create occasions of expense in religion"
  • • "Personal employment in serving Christ"
  • • "Forward to own Christ when others are shy"
  • • "Not condemn as waste what honors Christ"
21st Century Translations:
  • • Look for opportunities to invest in God's kingdom
  • • Serve personally, not just delegate or donate
  • • Boldly identify with Christ when it's unpopular
  • • Defend generous worship against "practical" criticism

📊 Contrasts in Christ's Kingship

Aspect Worldly Kings Christ the King Teaching Point
Transportation Chariots, war horses Young donkey True greatness is humble
Supporters Elite, wealthy Common people, pilgrims God values hearts over status
Purpose Conquest, power Salvation, service Leadership means serving others
Victory Through violence Through sacrifice True victory costs us personally
📖 Henry's Insight on Prophetic Fulfillment:

"Even this minute circumstance was foretold, and Christ took care it should be punctually fulfilled. Christ is Zion's king; the holy hill of Zion was destined to be the metropolis of the Messiah."

What This Teaches Us:
  • • God keeps promises down to small details
  • • Scripture has layers of fulfillment
  • • Christ actively fulfills prophecy
  • • Nothing in God's plan is accidental
Personal Application:
  • • Trust God's promises for your life
  • • Look for God's hand in small details
  • • Study Old Testament promises
  • • Expect God to be faithful
🧠 Understanding Spiritual Growth:

Henry notes: "They understood not these things at first... but when Jesus was glorified, then remembered they that these things were written of him."

📚 The Learning Progression:
  1. Experience without understanding
  2. Spirit-given insight later
  3. Retrospective clarity
  4. Word and experience align
💡 Practical Implications:

Don't be discouraged if you don't understand everything immediately. God reveals His purposes progressively. Keep participating faithfully even when you don't see the full picture.

🌾 The Grain of Wheat Principle (v.24)

🔍 Henry's Explanation:

"Christ is the corn of wheat, the most valuable and useful grain. He would never have been the living quickening head and root of the church if he had not descended from heaven to this accursed earth."

The Process:
  1. Seed must fall to the ground
  2. Seed must die (be buried)
  3. Death produces multiplication
  4. One becomes many
📝 Personal Applications:
In Ministry:

We must die to selfish ambitions to see spiritual fruit in others' lives.

In Relationships:

Sacrificing our preferences can strengthen and multiply healthy relationships.

In Character:

Dying to pride and self-centeredness produces Christlike virtues.

In Service:

Giving up comfort and convenience multiplies our effectiveness for God.

🎯 Henry's Clarification:

"Our life in this world includes all the enjoyments of our present state, riches, honours, pleasures, and long life in the possession of them; these we must hate, that is, despise them as vain and insufficient to make us happy."

❌ What "Loving Life" Means:
  • • Making comfort your highest priority
  • • Avoiding all risks for God's kingdom
  • • Choosing safety over obedience
  • • Living for temporary pleasures only
✅ What "Hating Life" Means:
  • • Valuing eternal things above temporal
  • • Willingness to sacrifice for God's purposes
  • • Choosing God's will over personal preference
  • • Living with eternity in view
🔄 The Paradox Explained:

Those who grasp tightly to this life lose what they're trying to preserve. Those who hold this life loosely, willing to lose it for Christ, gain both abundant life now and eternal life later.

👥 Framework for Following and Serving Christ (v.26)

1. Follow Christ's Methods

Do the things that He says, follow His example and pattern

  • • Study Christ's responses to different situations
  • • Practice compassion in daily interactions
  • • Prioritize prayer and time with God
  • • Serve the overlooked and marginalized
2. Follow Christ's Movements

Go where He leads, following His providence and Spirit

  • • Be sensitive to God's leading in circumstances
  • • Stay flexible with your plans when God redirects
  • • Participate where Christ's work is happening
  • • Be available for unexpected ministry opportunities
3. Share Christ's Presence

Where He is, there His servants should be also

  • • Regular participation in corporate worship
  • • Set your mind on things above (Col. 3:1-2)
  • • Cultivate awareness of Christ's presence
  • • Live with heaven's values in earthly situations

🔊 The Voice from Heaven (John 12:27-36)

😰 Christ's Soul Troubled - A Model for Our Struggles

🔍 Henry's Insight:

"A strange word to come from Christ's mouth... but the sin of our soul was the trouble of Christ's soul, when he undertook to redeem and save us."

The Process Christ Models:
  1. Acknowledge the struggle (v.27a)
  2. Pray to the Father (v.27b)
  3. Remember your calling (v.27c)
  4. Choose God's glory (v.28)
🎯 Applications for Our Struggles:
When Facing Difficult Decisions:

It's normal to feel internal conflict. Follow Christ's pattern: acknowledge it, pray about it, remember your purpose, choose God's glory.

When Suffering for Righteousness:

Christ's trouble validates your struggle. The goal isn't to avoid all difficulty but to glorify God through it.

When Feeling Overwhelmed:

Jesus himself felt overwhelmed yet remained obedient. Your feelings don't disqualify you from faithful service.

🎙️ Analysis of God's Response:

Henry notes: "When we pray as we are taught, 'Our Father, hallowed be thy name,' this is a comfort to us, that it is an answered prayer; answered to Christ here, and in him to all true believers."

Past Glorification:
  • • Through Christ's life and character
  • • Through His teachings and miracles
  • • Through examples of holiness and goodness
  • • Through fulfilled prophecies
Future Glorification:
  • • Through Christ's death and resurrection
  • • Through satisfaction of divine justice
  • • Through the salvation of many souls
  • • Through the establishment of His kingdom
💡 Why This Voice Came:

Christ explained: "This voice came not because of me, but for your sakes" (v.30). The voice served multiple purposes:

  • • To confirm Christ's divine mission to the crowd
  • • To encourage the disciples for coming trials
  • • To demonstrate the Father's approval
  • • To provide hope for all future believers

⚖️ "Now is the Judgment of This World" (v.31)

🔍 Henry's Explanation of "Judgment":

"The death of Christ was the judgment of this world" - This represents several types of judgment working simultaneously:

Discovery & Distinction

People's true character revealed by their response to the cross

Favor & Absolution

God's chosen ones receive forgiveness through Christ's sacrifice

Condemnation of Evil

Satan's dominion exposed as illegitimate usurpation

👿 Satan Cast Out - What This Means:

The "prince of this world" loses his authority through Christ's sacrificial death:

  • • His claim on humanity through sin is broken
  • • His power over death is destroyed
  • • His deception of nations is exposed
  • • His final defeat is assured
🎯 Drawing All People (v.32) - The Magnetic Cross:

Henry notes the paradox: what enemies thought would drive people away actually draws them. The lifted-up Christ becomes a spiritual magnet, drawing souls from every nation to salvation.

⚖️ The Pattern of Belief and Unbelief (John 12:37-43)

📜 John 12:37: "But although He had done so many signs before them, they did not believe in Him."

📊 Analyzing Responses to Christ's Ministry

🎯 Henry's Analysis of Unbelief:
Despite Abundant Evidence:

"He did miracles, so many miracles... numerous and often repeated... before many witnesses"

Fulfilling Isaiah's Prophecy:

"Who has believed our report? And to whom has the arm of the Lord been revealed?"

The Hidden Believers:

"Many even among the rulers believed in Him, but did not confess Him"

😔 Henry's Diagnosis of Their Problem:

"They loved the praise of men more than the praise of God... They set these two in the scale one against the other, and, having weighed them, they proceeded accordingly."

What They Valued:
  • • Praise from men
  • • Status among peers
  • • Career advancement
  • • Avoiding rejection
What They Sacrificed:
  • • Praise from God
  • • Personal integrity
  • • Gospel witness
  • • Eternal rewards
⚠️ Modern Applications - Where We See This Today:
In Professional Settings:
  • • Hiding Christian beliefs to avoid career consequences
  • • Staying silent when ungodly practices are promoted
  • • Compromising biblical values for business success
  • • Avoiding association with openly Christian colleagues
In Social Circles:
  • • Not sharing faith to maintain social acceptance
  • • Participating in activities that compromise convictions
  • • Avoiding church involvement to prevent judgment
  • • Downplaying religious commitment in conversations
✅ The Solution - Authentic Faith Expression:

Henry reminds us: "There is cause to question the sincerity of that faith which is either afraid or ashamed to show itself."

Practical Steps:
  • • Find appropriate ways to share your faith
  • • Be honest about your convictions when asked
  • • Support other Christians publicly when possible
  • • Prioritize God's approval over human approval
Remember:
  • • You may have more support than you think
  • • Your courage can inspire others to speak up
  • • Eternal rewards outweigh temporary losses
  • • God honors those who honor Him publicly

🔒 Understanding "They Could Not Believe" (v.39)

🤔 Henry's Careful Approach:

"This is a hard saying, who can explain it? We are sure that God is infinitely just and merciful... God dams none by mere sovereignty."

Two Types of "Could Not":
  1. Moral Impotency: They would not because they were obstinately resolved in their infidelity
  2. Judicial Consequence: God withdrew grace from those who persistently resisted it
The Process Henry Describes:
  1. God provides light and opportunity
  2. People persistently resist and rebel
  3. God justly withdraws further grace
  4. Hearts become hardened through their own choices
  5. What began as "will not" becomes "cannot"
⚠️ Warning for Today:

This serves as a caution to respond to God's grace while it's available. Persistent resistance to truth can lead to spiritual insensitivity and eventual inability to respond.

📢 Christ's Final Public Declaration (John 12:44-50)

📜 John 12:44-45: "Then Jesus cried out and said, 'He who believes in Me, believes not in Me but in Him who sent Me. And he who sees Me sees Him who sent Me.'"

Click to read full passage context

👑 Christ's Ultimate Authority Claims

🎯 What Christ Claims About Himself:
Divine Representative (v.44-45)

Believing in Him = believing in the Father; seeing Him = seeing the Father

Light of the World (v.46)

Came to deliver people from spiritual darkness

Perfect Messenger (v.49-50)

Speaks exactly what the Father commanded Him to speak

⚖️ What He Claims About Judgment:
Present Patience (v.47)

"I judge him not" - showing mercy and giving time for repentance

Future Judgment (v.48)

His words will judge unbelievers on the last day

Eternal Life Available (v.50)

God's commandment through Christ leads to everlasting life

🎙️ Henry's Interpretation of Christ's Crying Out:

"The raising of his voice and crying intimate... his boldness in speaking... his earnestness in speaking... his desire that all might take notice of it."

1. Boldness

Despite opposition, Christ courageously proclaimed truth publicly

2. Earnestness

He was serious and importunate, demonstrating genuine concern for souls

3. Universal Appeal

This being His last public address, He wanted everyone to hear

🎯 Modern Application for Christian Witness:
When to Speak with Urgency:
  • • When opportunities are limited
  • • When people's eternal destiny is at stake
  • • When cultural pressure tries to silence truth
  • • When speaking God's final word on a matter
How to Maintain Christ's Balance:
  • • Bold but not harsh
  • • Urgent but not panicked
  • • Public but not self-promoting
  • • Clear but not condemning

📋 Perfect Obedience to Divine Commission (v.49-50)

💼 Henry's Analysis of Christ's Commission:

"God the Father gave him his commission... like those given to an ambassador, directing him not only what he may say, but what he must say."

🎯 Christ's Perfect Faithfulness:
  • • Spoke exactly what the Father commanded
  • • Added nothing of His own human opinion
  • • Omitted nothing that was profitable
  • • Delivered the whole truth faithfully
📝 Our Application:
  • • Study to know God's will accurately
  • • Speak what God has revealed, not personal opinions
  • • Be faithful in both content and manner
  • • Remember we represent God in our words
♾️ The Ultimate Goal - Everlasting Life:

Henry notes: "I know that his commandment is life everlasting... knowing very well that he went upon a good errand, and that which would bring forth fruit unto life eternal."

Christ's perfect obedience was motivated by knowing His mission would result in eternal life for many. This gives us confidence that every word of Christ is trustworthy and leads to life.

📜 John 12:1-11 - Mary's Anointing

1 Then, six days before the Passover, Jesus came to Bethany, where Lazarus was who had been dead, whom He had raised from the dead.

2 There they made Him a supper; and Martha served, but Lazarus was one of those who sat at the table with Him.

3 Then Mary took a pound of very costly oil of spikenard, anointed the feet of Jesus, and wiped His feet with her hair. And the house was filled with the fragrance of the oil.

4 But one of His disciples, Judas Iscariot, Simon's son, who would betray Him, said,

5 "Why was this fragrant oil not sold for three hundred denarii and given to the poor?"

6 This he said, not that he cared for the poor, but because he was a thief, and had the money box; and he used to take what was put in it.

7 But Jesus said, "Let her alone; she has kept this for the day of My burial.

8 For the poor you have with you always, but Me you do not have always."

9 Now a great many of the Jews knew that He was there; and they came, not for Jesus' sake only, but that they might also see Lazarus, whom He had raised from the dead.

10 But the chief priests plotted to put Lazarus to death also,

11 because on account of him many of the Jews went away and believed in Jesus.

Henry's Key Insight: "Mary's love appears to have been generous, condescending, and believing. Those who love Christ truly love him better than this world."
