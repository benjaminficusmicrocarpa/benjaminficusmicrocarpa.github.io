üéØ The 65-20-15 Money Framework

The Three Numbers Everyone Should Know

This framework divides your net income (after-tax take-home pay) into three strategic buckets that create financial stability and freedom.

65%
Fundamentals
Essential living costs: rent, mortgage, utilities, groceries, minimum debt payments, car payments
20%
Fun Money
Guilt-free spending: restaurants, entertainment, hobbies, travel, spontaneous experiences
15%
Future You
Savings, investments, extra debt payments beyond minimums, wealth building
‚ö†Ô∏è

If You're Living Paycheck to Paycheck

These percentages might look different for you. If your essentials are taking 80-90% of your income, don't give up. Start with saving just 2-3% for your future. The habit matters more than the amount. As your income grows, you can adjust the ratios.

Remember: lifestyle inflation is your enemy. When you earn more, don't let your fundamentals percentage stay at 80% ‚Äî reduce it to 70%, then 65% as your income grows.

üìã The 4-Step Financial Foundation

üéØ Why This Order Matters

These steps are deliberately sequenced. Don't skip ahead to investing before you have your foundation. If you invest without an emergency fund and the market crashes, you'll be forced to sell at a loss or go into debt. Complete each step before moving to the next.

1Ô∏è‚É£

Build Your Peace of Mind Fund

Your psychological safety net

üéØ Target: 1 Month of Core Living Expenses

This isn't about mathematical optimization ‚Äî it's about psychology. Calculate your last 30 days of essential expenses (mortgage/rent, utilities, minimum debt payments, car payments). That total is your peace of mind fund.

Why This Matters:
  • ‚úì When your boiler breaks or car dies, you won't panic about paying for it
  • ‚úì You eliminate financial stress from life's inevitable curveballs
  • ‚úì Puts you ahead of 59% of Americans who can't cover a $1,000 expense
  • ‚úì Ahead of 30% of UK residents who can't cover one month's expenses

"Whatever life throws at me, I can handle it. That's what this covers. It's not optional, it's not for a holiday ‚Äî it's your financial airbag."

üìù How to Calculate It:
  1. Review your last 30 days of bank statements
  2. Add up ONLY essential expenses (rent/mortgage, utilities, food, minimum debt payments, transport)
  3. Exclude all discretionary spending (restaurants, entertainment, subscriptions)
  4. That total = your Peace of Mind Fund target
  5. Save this amount in a separate, easily accessible account
2Ô∏è‚É£

Cut the Financial Bleeding

Eliminate high-interest debt

‚ö†Ô∏è Priority: Debts Above 8% Interest Rate

Having savings earning 4% while carrying credit card debt at 20% is like pouring water into a bucket with holes. You're losing money faster than you're making it. This is the mathematical optimization step.

The Debt Avalanche Strategy:
  1. List all debts with their interest rates (credit cards, personal loans, car loans)
  2. Rank from highest to lowest interest rate
  3. Make minimum payments on everything (never miss a minimum payment)
  4. Throw all extra money at the highest interest debt first
  5. Once paid off, move to the next highest (the snowball effect)
Credit Card Smart Use:

Credit cards are ONLY beneficial if you pay them off in full every month. The rewards and points are worthless if you're paying 20% interest on the balance.

Rule: Only put on your credit card what you can pay for in cash today.

Credit card companies want you to miss payments ‚Äî that's how they make money. Don't fall into the trap. If you can't manage a credit card responsibly, cut it up until you can.

üí° Negotiation Tip:

Call your credit card company and negotiate a lower interest rate. Say: "I have a plan to pay off my debt over 12-18 months. Can you reduce my interest rate to help me achieve this?" Many will agree ‚Äî it costs nothing to ask.

3Ô∏è‚É£

Build Your Emergency Buffer

The ultimate financial security

üë§ Single: 3 Months üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß‚Äçüë¶ Head of Household: 6 Months

Take your core living expenses (from Step 1) and multiply by 3 (if single with predictable income) or 6 (if you have dependents, mortgage, or unpredictable income). This protects you from job loss, health scares, or major life disruptions.

üî¨ Research Finding (Vanguard Study):

Saving 3-6 months of living expenses does MORE for your emotional well-being than earning over $200,000. The breathing room, peace of mind, and security it provides is transformative.

This isn't just about numbers ‚Äî it's about freedom. Freedom to leave a toxic job. Freedom to take calculated risks. Freedom from the anxiety that keeps you up at night.

Benefits at Work:
  • ‚úì Productivity increases without financial stress
  • ‚úì Better decision-making from a place of security
  • ‚úì Negotiating power when you don't need the job
Benefits at Home:
  • ‚úì Relationship quality improves without money fights
  • ‚úì Mental health improves dramatically
  • ‚úì Sleep better knowing you're protected
üö´ Common Mistake to Avoid:

Don't count your "fun money" or discretionary spending in this calculation. If you lose your job, you won't be dining out or buying new clothes. This is SURVIVAL money only ‚Äî rent, utilities, food, minimum debt payments, essential transport.

4Ô∏è‚É£

Start Investing

Only after completing steps 1-3

‚è∞ Time is Your Greatest Asset
‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Warning:

Do NOT start investing before completing steps 1-3. If you invest without your emergency fund and the market drops 30%, you'll be forced to sell at a loss or go into debt when life happens.

The foundation isn't optional ‚Äî it's what allows you to weather market volatility without panic selling. Investing is a long-term game, and you can only play long-term if you're financially stable.

You cannot save your way to retirement anymore. With inflation averaging 3-4% and savings accounts paying 2-3%, you're actually losing purchasing power. Investing is essential, not optional.

"The phrase should be 'invest for retirement,' not 'save for retirement.' Saving is defense. Investing is offense. You need both, but you can't win the game playing only defense."

Two Main Investment Avenues:
üè¢
Employer Retirement Account

Automatically enrolled in UK (workplace pension), check with HR in US (401k). Pre-tax contributions that compound tax-free. Always contribute enough to get the full employer match ‚Äî it's literally free money with guaranteed 100% returns!

Example: If your employer matches 3%, contribute at least 3%. That's an instant 100% return before any market growth.

üíº
Individual Tax-Advantaged Account

ISA in UK (¬£20k annual limit), Roth IRA in US ($7-8k limit). After-tax contributions that grow tax-free and can be withdrawn tax-free. Max this out before any taxable investing.

If you invest ¬£20k at age 25 and it grows to ¬£100k by age 65, that entire ¬£80k gain is TAX-FREE in an ISA. In a taxable account, you'd pay capital gains tax on that ¬£80k.

üéØ Investment Priority Order:
  1. Contribute to employer plan up to the match (free money)
  2. Max out tax-advantaged accounts (ISA/Roth IRA)
  3. Increase employer plan contributions above the match
  4. Only then consider taxable investment accounts

üìà Investing Made Simple

Nischa's Investing Philosophy

"Keep it simple. Do it for the long term. Early and often."

üéØ What to Invest In

Index funds (S&P 500, FTSE 100) and target-date retirement funds

üìä Expected Returns

8-10% average annual returns over the long term (20+ years)

üìö What is an Index Fund?

Index: A list of companies (e.g., S&P 500 = top 500 US companies by market cap)

Fund: A pot of money that invests in those companies on your behalf

Result: By investing in an S&P 500 index fund, you own a small piece of 500 companies ‚Äî Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Tesla, and 495 others

Why This Works:

Even if one company fails (like Lehman Brothers in 2008), you're diversified across hundreds of others. The market trend is upward over time ‚Äî always has been, always will be.

Historical fact: The S&P 500 has never had a negative 20-year period. Ever. If you hold long enough, you win.

üéØ Target-Date Retirement Funds

These funds automatically rebalance as you approach retirement, becoming more conservative over time to protect your gains.

How to choose: Pick the year closest to when you plan to retire (e.g., Target 2050 Fund if retiring around 2050)

What it does: Starts 90% stocks/10% bonds when you're young, gradually shifts to 40% stocks/60% bonds as you approach retirement

Set It & Forget It:

Perfect for hands-off investors who want professional rebalancing without thinking about it. The fund managers do all the work for a tiny fee (usually 0.1-0.2%).

When fear and anxiety took over during market drops, people sold. When greed kicked in during market highs, they bought. They essentially bought high and sold low ‚Äî the exact opposite of what you should do. Their emotions destroyed their returns.

üéì Advanced Investing Concepts Simplified

Compound Interest: The Eighth Wonder

If you invest ¬£500/month at 10% annual return starting at age 25, by age 65 you'll have ¬£3.16 million. Total contributed: ¬£240,000. Total earned from growth: ¬£2.92 million.

The first ¬£240k you control. The ¬£2.92 million is compound interest working for you while you sleep. That's the power of time.

Dollar-Cost Averaging: Remove Emotion

Instead of trying to time the market (which professionals fail at), invest the same amount every month. When the market crashes, you're buying stocks "on sale." When it's high, you're still investing but buying fewer shares.

Over decades, this strategy has outperformed lump-sum investing for most people because it removes the temptation to panic sell or wait for the "perfect" time.

Diversification: Risk Management

An S&P 500 index fund gives you instant diversification across 500 companies, 11 sectors, and the entire US economy. If tech crashes but energy booms, you're balanced. If Apple fails but Microsoft thrives, you're protected.

Individual stock picking is gambling unless you have insider knowledge (which is illegal) or devote 60+ hours per week to research. Index funds give you the market average, which beats 90% of professional fund managers over 20 years.

üìà Strategy 1: Ask for a Raise (The Easiest Method)

üí° Nischa's Personal Raise Strategy

In her 9 years at banking, Nischa asked for raises multiple times. She increased her salary from roughly ¬£50k to ¬£220k using this exact approach.

üìã Her Exact Script:

"Hey [Manager], three/six months ago we discussed the things I needed to do to get promoted/get a raise. We mentioned X, Y, and Z. I've completed all of those, and here's the feedback I've received. Here's where I've gone above and beyond. Based on our conversation and the 360 feedback, do you think [the raise/promotion we discussed] is fair?"

Notice: She references a PREVIOUS conversation, provides EVIDENCE, and asks for THEIR opinion. This makes it collaborative, not confrontational.

üìù

Step 1: Build Your Case (Before the Conversation)

  • ‚úì Document your wins: Keep a running list of accomplishments, projects completed, revenue generated, costs saved
  • ‚úì Research market rates: Use Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary, or industry reports for your role, experience level, and location
  • ‚úì Ask HR about ranges: "Am I aligned to the average for my department and role?" They can tell you without revealing others' salaries
  • ‚úì Gather 360 feedback: Get testimonials from colleagues, clients, other managers about your contributions
  • ‚úì Align to company goals: Show how your work directly contributed to company objectives and revenue
üó£Ô∏è

Step 2: Have the Conversation (Timing & Delivery)

‚è∞ Best Timing:
  • ‚Ä¢ After a major win or successful project
  • ‚Ä¢ During annual review cycles
  • ‚Ä¢ When you've taken on new responsibilities
  • ‚Ä¢ NOT when the company just announced layoffs or budget cuts
üí¨ Conversation Framework:
  1. Start with gratitude: "I really appreciate the opportunities I've had here..."
  2. Reference previous discussions: "In our last review, we talked about X, Y, Z..."
  3. Present evidence: "Since then, I've accomplished A, B, C..."
  4. Show market data: "My research shows the market rate for this role is..."
  5. Make the ask: "Based on this, I'd like to discuss a raise to [specific number]"
  6. Ask for their perspective: "What are your thoughts on this?"
