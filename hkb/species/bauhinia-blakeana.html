Bauhinia × blakeana

Hong Kong Orchid Tree 洋紫荊 · 白花洋紫荊

Dunn

Taxonomic Classification 分類學分類

Kingdom界 Plantae
Division門 Magnoliophyta
Class綱 Magnoliopsida
Order目 Fabales
Family科 Caesalpiniaceae
Genus屬 Bauhinia
Species種 B. blakeana

Plant morphology 植物形態

General Habit 一般習性

Growth Form生長形態

Tree, reaching 8–10 meters in height.

喬木，高達 8–10 米。

Bark & Branches

Dark brown and fissured bark; young branches have fine, short hairs.

樹皮深褐色且有縫裂；幼枝具短柔毛。

Special Notes備註

Originally discovered in Pok Fu Lam, Hong Kong; named to commemorate Sir Henry and Lady Blake.

最初由巴黎外方傳教會神父於香港薄扶林海邊發現；種加詞為紀念卜力爵士伉儷。

Leaves 葉

Shape形狀

Circular to broad heart-shaped, distinctly divided into two lobes (bifid) at the tip.

近圓形或廣心形，先端明顯二裂。

Size大小

Roughly 8.5–13 cm long and 9–14 cm wide.

長約 8.5–13 厘米，闊 9–14 厘米。

Texture & Color

Papery to slightly leathery; smooth on top, sparsely hairy underneath.

紙質至略帶革質；葉面無毛，葉底疏生短柔毛。

Special Notes備註

Often referred to colloquially as a "camel's foot" leaf due to its distinct shape.

因其獨特的形狀，常被俗稱為「羊蹄甲」。

Flowers 花

Appearance外觀

Showy, bilateral symmetry with 5 oblanceolate petals.

花朵鮮豔，兩側對稱，具 5 枚倒披針形花瓣。

Color顏色

Red to violet.

紅色至紫羅蘭色。

Stamens雄蕊

5 fertile stamens and 2–5 sterile staminodes.

5 枚可育雄蕊，2–5 枚退化雄蕊。

Flowering Period花期

Blooms almost all year round.

花期近乎全年。

Special Notes備註

Adopted as the floral emblem of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region in 1997.

於 1997 年成為香港特別行政區的區花。

Fruits 果

Type & Characteristics

Legume; dry, dehiscent, and strap-shaped (lorate).

莢果；乾果，開裂，帶狀。

Color顏色

Pink to brown.

粉紅色至褐色。

Size大小

15–25 cm long, 1.5–2 cm in diameter.

長 15–25 厘米，直徑 1.5–2 厘米。

Fruiting Period果期

Documented year-round.

果期近乎全年。

Special Notes備註

Pods are usually not produced as this species is a sterile hybrid.

通常不結果，因其為天然不育雜交種。

Ecology 生態學

  • Functions as a sterile hybrid of Bauhinia purpurea and Bauhinia variegata, generating no seed pods and presenting a minimal ecological invasion risk.
  • Requires vegetative propagation strictly through grafting, cuttings, or air-layering due to its sterility.
  • Thrives in USDA Hardiness Zones 9a to 11, tolerating minimum temperatures between -3.9 C and -3.3 C before sustaining severe foliage damage.
  • Flourishes in full sun to partial shade and adapts to slightly acidic to alkaline loam, sand, and clay soils provided there is adequate drainage.
  • Attracts pollinators including bees, butterflies, and hummingbirds.
  • Serves as a targeted nectar source for regional avifauna, specifically the Fork-tailed Sunbird (Aethopyga christinae).

Landscape Architecture 景觀設計

  • Attains a mature height of 6.1 m to 12.2 m with an irregular, umbrella-like canopy spread of 6.1 m to 7.6 m.
  • Produces 15.2 cm to 20.3 cm bilobed, gray-green leaves and prominent 12.7 cm to 15.2 cm fragrant, purplish-red to pink sterile blossoms containing five stamens.
  • Provides extensive seasonal color from November to March when other landscape plants typically lack blooms.
  • Requires a minimum planting distance of 2.4 m to 3.0 m from adjacent structures, sidewalks, and driveways to accommodate canopy spread and minimize clearance issues.
  • Functions optimally as a street tree, shade tree, or standalone specimen in medium-to-large landscapes.

Urban Forestry 城市林業

  • Exhibits a fast to medium growth rate, adding 30 cm to 60 cm of height per year until maturity.
  • Demonstrates high drought tolerance once established and performs effectively in highly urbanized, pollution-heavy environments.
  • Possesses brittle wood and a weak structural crotch, making branches highly susceptible to breakage during storms; therefore, avoid planting in high-wind zones.
  • Mandates proactive structural pruning during the first five years to establish a central dominant trunk and prevent multi-stem structural failures.
  • Requires active pest management for Chinese rose beetles, borers, caterpillars, and mites, which primarily cause foliar damage.

Phytosociology 植群生態學

  • Faces growth inhibition when competing with allelopathic turfgrasses (e.g., tall fescue, Kentucky bluegrass), which can disrupt tree nutrient uptake and mycorrhizal root development.
  • Benefits from organic mulching and turfgrass suppression extending beyond the dripline to facilitate optimal fine-root development and minimize stress-induced allelochemical susceptibility.
  • Presents elevated phytotoxic vulnerability in poorly drained clay soils where allelochemicals accumulate, compared to well-drained sandy soils where natural leaching occurs.
  • Pairs successfully as an overstory canopy with compatible understory species such as Asiatic jasmine, dwarf oyster plant, Liriope, Japanese boxwood, and foxtail fern.

Ethnobotany 民族植物學

  • Adopted as the official floral emblem of Hong Kong in 1965, visibly represented on the regional flag and currency.
  • Revered culturally for its bilobed "clever leaf," utilized traditionally as bookmarks to symbolize wisdom and bring academic luck.
  • Applied in folk medicine systems globally to treat pain-related diseases, serving as a functional analgesic and anti-inflammatory agent.
  • Exhibits potent antibacterial, haemostatic, diuretic, and antidiabetic characteristics.
  • Contains high concentrations of bioactive compounds, including bioflavonoids, tannins, triterpenoids, and amino acids, which yield significant antioxidant and alpha-glucosidase inhibitory qualities for diabetes treatment.
