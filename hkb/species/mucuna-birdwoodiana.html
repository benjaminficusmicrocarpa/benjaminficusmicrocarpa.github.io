Mucuna birdwoodiana

Birdwood's Mucuna 禾雀花 · 白花油麻藤

Tutcher

Taxonomic Classification 分類學分類

Kingdom界 Plantae
Division門 Magnoliophyta
Class綱 Magnoliopsida
Order目 Fabales
Family科 Fabaceae
Genus屬 Mucuna
Species種 M. birdwoodiana

Plant morphology 植物形態

General Habit 一般習性

Growth Form生長形態

Large woody climber.

大型木質藤本。

Stem Characteristics

Exudes white juice when hurt that quickly turns red; transverse section of stem is red-brown with 3–4 obliquely concentric circles.

受傷時先流出白色汁液，隨後迅速變紅；莖橫切面呈紅褐色，具 3–4 個斜向的同心圓環。

Special Notes備註

Common in Hong Kong forests. Type specimen was collected from Mount Parker by W. J. Tutcher in 1903. Medicinal and ornamental uses.

常見於香港林地。模式標本由 W. J. Tutcher 於 1903 年在柏架山採集。具藥用及觀賞價值。

Leaves 葉

Shape形狀

Pinnately 3-foliolate. Terminal leaflets elliptic, ovate, or ± obovate with rounded or cuneate base and caudate-acuminate apex; lateral leaflets oblique at base.

羽狀三出複葉。頂生小葉呈橢圓形、卵形或略倒卵形，基部圓形或楔形，先端尾狀漸尖；側生小葉基部偏斜。

Size大小

Terminal leaflets 3–16 cm long and 2–6 cm wide; acumen (tip) 1.3–2 cm.

頂生小葉長 3–16 厘米，闊 2–6 厘米；漸尖頭長 1.3–2 厘米。

Texture質地

Subleathery; glabrous or sparsely pubescent.

近革質；無毛或疏生短柔毛。

Flowers 花

Inflorescence花序

Axillary racemes or borne on old branches, 20–38 cm long, with 2–3 flowers on each node. Campanulate calyx with rusty pubescence.

總狀花序腋生或生於老枝上，長 20–38 厘米，每節具 2–3 朵花。鐘狀花萼被鏽色短柔毛。

Corolla Appearance

Greyish white. Vexillum ovate-orbicular (3.5–4 cm); wings (6–7 cm); keel narrowly winged and brown pubescent (7.5–8.7 cm).

花冠灰白色。旗瓣卵狀圓形（長 3.5–4 厘米）；翼瓣（長 6–7 厘米）；龍骨瓣具狹翅及棕色短柔毛（長 7.5–8.7 厘米）。

Flowering Period花期

April to June.

4 月至 6 月。

Fruits 果

Type & Characteristics

Leathery, loriform (strap-shaped) pod, densely rusty-pubescent, with 2 narrow wings along sutures, slightly constricted between seeds.

革質帶狀莢果，密被鏽色短柔毛，沿縫線具 2 條狹翅，種子間微縊縮。

Size & Seed Count

30–45 cm long, 3.5–4 cm wide. Contains 5–13 seeds.

長 30–45 厘米，闊 3.5–4 厘米。內含 5–13 顆種子。

Fruiting Period果期

June to November.

6 月至 11 月。

Seeds 種子

Appearance & Size

Suborbicular, purple-black, shining. 2.8–3 cm long and 2–2.5 cm wide. Poisonous.

近圓形，紫黑色具光澤。長 2.8–3 厘米，闊 2–2.5 厘米。有毒。

Ecology 生態學

  • Native to Hong Kong and South China (Fujian, Guangdong, Guangxi, Guizhou, Jiangxi, Sichuan) in dense or open woodlands, rocky areas, and moist valleys at altitudes of 800–2500 m.
  • Primary pollinators are non-flying mammals, specifically Masked palm civets (Paguma larvata) and Pallas's squirrels (Callosciurus erythraeus styani).
  • Reproduction relies strictly on "explosive opening" of petals triggered by physical manipulation from mammals; unopened flowers do not fructify.
  • Secretes sucrose-dominant nectar continuously, with no variation in nectar volume or sugar concentration throughout the day or night to maximize mammalian attraction.
  • Woody climbing vines grow up to 15 cm in diameter, providing sufficient structural support to carry arboreal mammals weighing up to 6 kg.

Landscape Architecture 景觀設計

  • Classified as a large, evergreen climbing vine suitable for hillside, slope, and hanging plant designs.
  • Produces hanging inflorescences 20–38 cm long, each bearing 12–40 pale green, yellow, or white flowers measuring 7.5–9 cm tall.
  • Foliage consists of leathery, glossy, glabrous leaves measuring 17–30 cm long with 8–20 cm petioles.
  • Flowering occurs from March to June, with fruit production from June to November.
  • Requires robust vertical supports capable of sustaining heavy, thick woody lianas up to 15 cm in diameter.
  • Produces woody legumes 30–45 cm long, 3.5–4.5 cm wide, and 1–1.5 cm thick, covered in fine, reddish-brown irritant hairs that require careful spatial planning away from contact zones.

Urban Forestry 城市林業

  • Best deployed in peripheral urban woodlands, ravines, and conservation parks rather than high-traffic pedestrian areas due to poisonous seeds and irritant pod hairs.
  • Functions as a critical biodiversity enhancer in urban patches, as its large nectar volumes sustain local non-flying urban mammal populations like civets and squirrels.
  • Requires existing mature canopy trees or heavy infrastructure to support vertical climbing habit.
  • Maintenance personnel must be aware that cutting or damaging the stem releases a white sap that rapidly oxidizes to a red-brown color.
  • Can aggressively festoon woodland canopies, requiring monitoring to prevent excessive structural loading on host trees.

Phytosociology 植群生態學

  • Belongs to the Fabaceae family, specifically placed in Mucuna subg. Macrocarpa, characterized by long ovaries, numerous seeds per legume, and linear, woody epicarps.
  • Dominates as a canopy liana in broadleaved evergreen forests and valley ecosystems across South China.
  • Shares overlapping spatial distribution and flowering phenology with sympatric species Mucuna championii in regions like Hong Kong, maintaining segregation through distinct pollinator interactions.
  • Lacks persistent stipels, separating it phenotypically from other regional vines.
  • Acts as a structural link between the understory and upper canopy, providing vital arboreal pathways.

Ethnobotany 民族植物學

  • Seeds are traditionally processed for medicinal applications to treat joint pain and irregular menstruation.
  • Utilized historically as a source of starch, though seeds are noted as poisonous and require specific local processing.
  • Colloquially known as the "Rice Bird Flower" (禾雀花) because its floral morphology-pale yellow vexillum, lateral wings, and curled tail-like petals-mimics the Yellow-breasted Bunting (Emberiza aureola).
  • Tied to Chinese folklore involving Iron-Crutch Li of the Eight Immortals, corresponding its blooming period with the Ching Ming Festival.
  • The stems yield a red-brown transverse section with 3-4 obliquely concentric circles, historically noted by local gatherers.
