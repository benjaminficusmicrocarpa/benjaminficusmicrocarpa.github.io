Morphological Description 形態描述

A spectacular semi-evergreen to evergreen shrub, 1–3 m tall, with elliptic to obovate leaves 2–6 cm long, densely covered with appressed brown hairs on both surfaces when young, becoming glabrescent above with age. The leaf margins are entire, and the petioles are 3–6 mm long, clothed in strigose hairs.

Flowers are funnel-shaped, 3–6 cm across, typically bright red to deep pink (occasionally pale pink or white in cultivated forms), appearing in clusters of 2–6 at branch tips. The corolla is broadly funnel-shaped with five spreading lobes, often spotted with darker markings on the upper lobe. Stamens 10, exserted; ovary densely strigose. Capsule ovoid, 8–12 mm long, covered with appressed brown hairs, splitting into five valves at maturity.

Found abundantly on acidic hillsides and open shrubland from 200–900 m elevation across Hong Kong, particularly prominent on Ma On Shan, Tai Mo Shan, and Sunset Peak during February to April. This species is one of the most iconic wildflowers of the Hong Kong spring and is the progenitor of many ornamental azalea cultivars grown worldwide.

Distribution & Habitat 分佈與生境

Rhododendron simsii is widely distributed across East Asia, from eastern Myanmar through southern and central China to Taiwan and Japan. In Hong Kong, it is one of the most commonly encountered native shrubs on upland acidic hillsides, typically found on well-drained, nutrient-poor, granitic or volcanic soils.

Key populations occur along the ridgelines and upper slopes of Ma On Shan (馬鞍山), the flanks of Tai Mo Shan (大帽山), the summit plateau of Sunset Peak (大東山) on Lantau, and the exposed slopes above Sai Kung (西貢). It is a characteristic component of the Rhodomyrtus–Baeckea shrubland community that dominates fire-maintained hillsides between 300 and 800 m altitude.

The species favours acidic soils (pH 4.5–5.5) and tolerates moderate drought once established, though it thrives in the misty, humid conditions typical of Hong Kong's upland environments during the cooler months.
