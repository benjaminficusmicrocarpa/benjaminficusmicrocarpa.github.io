üìñ Introducing "The Way"

The early church wasn't originally called "the church" ‚Äî it was called the Way. This title traces directly to Jesus himself, who provocatively declared in John 14:6: "I am the way and the truth and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me."

This claim is exclusive and radical: not a way, but the way. Not all roads lead to the same mountaintop. Jesus uniquely, because of who He was, the life He led, and most importantly His power over death ‚Äî His resurrection ‚Äî positioned Himself as the bridge back to a holy God.

In Acts, the Apostle Paul refers to the church as "the Way" five times ‚Äî both as Saul the persecutor and later as Paul the believer. Standing before Festus on trial, he would say: "I am a follower of the Way."

Lent Series: For the next six weeks leading up to Easter, this series dives into Scripture to consider what it means to follow the way of Jesus ‚Äî His life, His ministry, the claims He made, and the uniqueness of the gospel.

üé£ Jesus Calls the Twelve

Why Jesus Avoided the Crowds

As Jesus began His public ministry around age 30, He healed many people in miraculous ways. Naturally, His reputation grew and massive crowds followed. You'd think this is exactly what a revolutionary leader would want.

But oddly, Jesus repudiated the crowds. He even had a small boat ready as an escape plan. Why? Because the people were coming for the wrong reason. They sought the healing more than the healer. The physical had taken over the spiritual.

Make no mistake, God cares about your physical well-being. You are created in the image of God with a physical body. But the greater tragedy is when we focus only on the physical at the expense of the spiritual.

Jesus' mission was to seek and to save the lost ‚Äî to make sure people understood their spiritual condition before a holy God. There were only two options: live a perfect life (impossible) or trust in a perfect Savior in Jesus.

The whole Godhead ‚Äî Father, Son, and Holy Spirit ‚Äî is relational. In Genesis 1, before mankind existed, they have a conversation: "Let us make mankind in our image." "Us" and "our" ‚Äî a plurality within the Godhead, community and relationship existing even before creation.
