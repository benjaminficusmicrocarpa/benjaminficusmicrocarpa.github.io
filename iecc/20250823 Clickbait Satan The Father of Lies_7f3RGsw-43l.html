🐍 Genesis 3: The Original Deception

🗣️ God said: Genesis 2:16-17 (NIV): "And the Lord God commanded the man, 'You are free to eat from any tree in the garden; but you must not eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, for when you eat from it you will certainly die.'" "You are free to eat from any tree in the garden, but you must not eat from the tree of knowledge of good and evil."

🐍 Satan said: Genesis 3:1 (NIV): "Now the serpent was more crafty than any of the wild animals the Lord God had made. He said to the woman, 'Did God really say, "You must not eat from any tree in the garden"?'" "Did God really say you must not eat from ANY tree in the garden?"

Satan's Strategy: 🔄 Corrupt God's word → 🚫 Contradict God's word Genesis 3:4-5 (NIV): "'You will not certainly die,' the serpent said to the woman. 'For God knows that when you eat from it your eyes will be opened, and you will be like God, knowing good and evil.'" → 💭 Capture the heart & mind

The Real Goal: Satan wanted to engage Eve in conversation, making her feel it was safe to dialogue with him - giving him a foothold!

🍎 The Result: Genesis 3:6 (NIV): "When the woman saw that the fruit of the tree was good for food and pleasing to the eyes, and also desirable for gaining wisdom, she took some and ate it. She also gave some to her husband, who was with her, and he ate it." Eve ate and gave to Adam

😤 The Blame Game: Genesis 3:12 (NIV): "The man said, 'The woman you put here with me—she gave me some fruit from the tree, and I ate it.'" Adam blamed both Eve AND God!

🔍 Discernment of Spirits (Ignatius of Loyola)

😈 For Ungodly People

Satan's Tactic: Proposes apparent pleasures, leading them to IMAGINE sensual delights Ignatius (quoted by Pastor Albert): "In persons who are going from mortal sin to mortal sin, the enemy is ordinarily accustomed to propose apparent pleasures to them, leading them to imagine sensual delight and pleasures in order to hold them more and make them grow in their vices and sins."

Result: Over-promise, under-deliver - addiction cycle

Holy Spirit: Stings and bites the conscience Ignatius (quoted by Pastor Albert): "In these persons, the good spirit uses a contrary method, stinging and biting the conscience through their rational power of moral judgment." , convicting them

😇 For Godly People

Satan's Tactic: Discouragement - bites, creates obstacles, false reasons Ignatius (quoted by Pastor Albert): "For then it is proper to the evil spirit to bite Satan and place obstacles, disquieting with false reasons so that the person may not go forward."

Goal: Prevent you from getting closer to God

Holy Spirit: Gives courage, strength, consolation, and clarity Ignatius (quoted by Pastor Albert): "It is proper to the good spirits to give courage and strength, consolations, tears, and quiet. Easing and taking away all obstacles so that the person you may go forward in doing good."
