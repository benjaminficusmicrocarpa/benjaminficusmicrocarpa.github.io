üìñ The Message: Don't Throw Away Your Confidence

üçï The Pineapple Pizza Problem

Hawaiian pizza ‚Äî that deliciously divisive topic ‚Äî was actually invented in 1962 by a Greek-Canadian chef in Ontario, not in Hawaii. He simply named it after the pineapple can label. A study of 34,000 people found 54% opposed it, 12% call it their favorite topping, and 24% call it their least favorite. In 2017, the President of Iceland even joked he'd outlaw it ‚Äî and had to publicly apologize when the backlash got too intense.

The point? We all have things we like and things we don't. And normally, that's fine ‚Äî you can pick and choose. But when it comes to Scripture, there are things you may not like that you would be a fool to avoid.

Thomas Jefferson's Bible: The third U.S. president famously took scissors to his Bible and cut out the verses he didn't like. You and I probably don't use scissors, but we might just turn the page ‚Äî looking for something that feels good, a promise we want to claim, while skipping the hard parts.

‚öì Don't Throw Away Your Confidence

"Above all else, guard your heart" ‚Äî the theme of this Soul Garden series. One way to guard your heart is to be guided by wisdom greater than yourself. As smart and educated as you may be, there is still a whole lot you do not know. God's wisdom is greater than yours.

What do you do when there's nothing you can do? When circumstances come that throw you for a loop ‚Äî relationship breakdowns, job losses, financial pressure, health crises ‚Äî things beyond your control that leave you asking, "What do I do when I don't know what to do?"

üìñ Hebrews 10:35-36 (NIV)

What is this "confidence"? Not self-confidence. Not self-esteem. Not your own accolades. It's your confidence in God ‚Äî your faith. Your belief in a God who is in control and always has been.

üí° The Word "Persevere"

The English word comes from Latin: per (through) + severe (harsh, painful). To persevere is literally to go through circumstances that feel severe. Scripture doesn't just offer a cheerful "keep going!" ‚Äî it offers a reward:

  • There's purpose in your pain
  • There's something waiting that will make it worthwhile
  • Should you persevere, you will look back and say, "Oh, I see. I understand now what you were doing, God."

üåü James on Trials and Perseverance

‚è∞ "Whenever" ‚Äî Not "If"

James doesn't say if you face trials, but whenever. It's a matter of when, not if. The Greek word for "face" carries an element of surprise ‚Äî like being ambushed by a thief. When you are surprised by a phone call, a conversation, an email, a stock market crash ‚Äî something that disrupts your peace and you didn't see coming.

ü§î "Consider It" ‚Äî Stop and Reframe

Without a pause, our natural response to a trial is a defense strategy ‚Äî research, networking, action plans. But James says: before you start your action plan, consider. Think about what God is doing. Why has this happened? What might God be teaching my soul?

Consider it pure joy, my brothers and sisters, whenever you face trials of many kinds.

‚Äî James 1:2

"Consider it what? Pure joy?!" This is the pineapple pizza of Scripture. We want to argue: "Are you kidding me? That's irrational. You're out of touch. You're hyper-spiritual. You're in denial." And yet James says it to those who have a faith in Christ ‚Äî those who have a supernatural capacity to see beyond the natural.

üî• "Because You Know" ‚Äî You're Already Informed

When faced with difficulty, we think we need something new ‚Äî more information, a new doctor, a new strategy. James says: you already know. Because of your faith, you're already informed about who God is, who you are, and the expectations you can have in this life.

‚ö° Testing Produces Perseverance

The "testing of your faith" does two things:

  1. Proves it is authentic ‚Äî Like a lighter test on a leather wallet in Shenzhen. It's easy to believe in a faith that is only lollipops and sunshine. Trials settle out whether you're really in it.
  2. Produces perseverance ‚Äî Spiritual grit that builds inside you. You're going to need it, because another trial is coming.
"Let perseverance finish its work" ‚Äî Don't quit the race. Don't throw in the towel. The thing you want removed is often the thing God wants to use. It grabs your heart, brings the tears, keeps you awake at night. And God says: "Trust me. I know what to do."

üèÜ The Result: Mature and Complete

We think we lack ‚Äî "if only I had this, if only you'd remove that." But persevering in faith takes you to a place of maturity where you recognize: "I have all that I need. God is sufficient. He is enough."

üôè Ask for Wisdom

James catches us right where we are. In the midst of crying out to God for relief, what we really need is wisdom. An admission of the gap between your finite wisdom and God's infinite wisdom is a great starting point.

üìñ James 1:5 (NIV)

God doesn't hold back reluctantly. He's not a God who says "just keep praying, I'll see." When you ask, He gives. He wants you to understand His purpose in this world and in your life.

The church James led was not a wealthy, comfortable congregation:

üéØ Practical Application

‚öì When You Don't Know What to Do

Life brings circumstances beyond our control ‚Äî relationship breakdowns, career upheavals, financial strain, health crises. We're a capable group; we relish solving problems. But some things are beyond our ability to fix.

üõë

Don't Abandon God

The temptation in severe trials is to either abandon faith altogether or take control from God. Both are throwing away your confidence. Instead, persevere ‚Äî keep trusting even when you can't see the outcome.

‚è∏Ô∏è

Pause and Consider

Before launching into your action plan, stop. Think about what God might be doing. Ask: "What might God be teaching me? How can I see His purpose in this?" Don't just react ‚Äî reflect.

üîÑ

Reframe the Trial

"Consider it pure joy" isn't about pretending. It's about recognizing, as a Christian, that you have a supernatural capacity to see purpose in pain. There is redemptive good alongside the heartache.

üôè

Ask for Wisdom

You're smart, degreed, accomplished ‚Äî but understanding the ways of God goes beyond a textbook or masterclass. Admit the gap between your finite mind and God's infinite wisdom, and ask.

üìã Don't Throw Away Your Confidence ‚Äî A Checklist

üö´ What NOT to Do in Trials

  • Don't treat God like a vending machine ‚Äî Press C4 and wait for the goodie. He's not Santa Claus.
  • Don't take scissors to Scripture ‚Äî Like Thomas Jefferson, we're tempted to cut out what's hard. But to avoid the full counsel of God is a foolish decision.
  • Don't let surprise steal your faith ‚Äî The Greek word for "face" in James 1:2 carries the idea of being caught off guard, like by a thief. Don't let the shock undo your trust in God.
  • Don't quit the race early ‚Äî "Let perseverance finish its work." The thing you want removed may be the very thing God wants to use.

‚úÖ What TO Do in Trials

  1. Remember what you already know ‚Äî Your faith already informs you about who God is and who you are. Don't act like you need to start from scratch.
  2. See the macro picture ‚Äî We get laser-focused on what's hard. God says: "It's hard, but also know that there is good being accomplished."
  3. Trust His heart when you can't trace His hand ‚Äî Like a loving father, God always desires what is good for His children.
  4. Persevere toward maturity ‚Äî The goal isn't just survival; it's becoming "mature and complete, not lacking anything."

When you cannot trace His hand, you can trust His heart. This God is good, and like a loving father who always desires what is good for his son, God the perfect Father even more has your good in mind.

üí≠ Reflection Questions

These questions are drawn from the sermon and the official YouTube reflection questions. Take time to thoughtfully consider them, perhaps journaling your responses or discussing with your care group.

  • What is the toughest circumstance in your life right now?

    Name it before God. How are you handling it ‚Äî with faith, with fear, or by trying to take control? What would it look like to persevere rather than abandon or control?

  • In what areas of your life have you shown determination and grit?

    Think about career, parenting, school, fitness. How can you apply that same level of perseverance in your walk with God?

  • Are there scriptures or teachings you've been "cutting out" because they're hard to accept?

    Like Thomas Jefferson's scissors or the pineapple pizza you'd rather avoid ‚Äî what parts of God's word have you been turning the page on? "Do not worry about tomorrow." "Forgive as the Lord forgave you." What else?

  • When a trial surprises you, what is your first instinct?

    Do you go straight to action mode? Research mode? Panic mode? James says: first, consider ‚Äî pause and think about what God might be doing before you launch into your solution.

  • Is your faith being tested right now, and what is it revealing?

    Trials reveal whether our faith is authentic or a "vending machine" approach. Are you following Jesus for who He is, or just for the blessings? What is the current testing showing you?

  • Is there a trial in your life where you've been praying for relief, but God may be asking you to let perseverance finish its work?

    The thing you want removed may be the thing God wants to use. Can you see any redemptive purpose in the difficulty you're facing?

  • Where do you need God's wisdom right now?

    He promises to give generously, without finding fault. Have you actually asked? What gap between your understanding and His do you need Him to bridge?

  • "When you cannot trace His hand, you can trust His heart." Do you believe this?

    In the areas where God's plan is unclear, can you still trust that He is good, that He is a perfect Father, and that He has your good in mind?
