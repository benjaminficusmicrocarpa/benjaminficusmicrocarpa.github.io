📋 Sermon Overview

Central Theme: Many Christians feel caught between two authorities — wanting to follow Jesus yet still pulled toward sin — and secretly wonder if they are hypocrites. Pastor Kevin walks through Romans 8:5–13: what the flesh is (disordered desires), the consequences of a mind set on the flesh versus the Spirit, the decisive difference between struggling with the flesh and living in the realm of the flesh, and our calling to put the misdeeds of the body to death through dedication, discipline, and Spirit-empowered means — not to earn salvation, but to fight the battle we are now free to fight.

Series context: Week 2 of seven in Romans 8. Last week, Pastor Brett opened with no condemnation (Romans 8:1–4). This week focuses on verses 5–13 — the hidden battle within and life under the Spirit's governance.

🌟 Main Takeaways

  1. The flesh is disordered desire — misplaced priorities (loving good things out of order) and disproportionate obsessions (good things becoming ultimate things).
  2. Two pathways for the mind — set on the flesh: death (deadened spiritual sensitivity), chaos, hostility to God's righteousness, and (for those in the realm of the flesh) inability to please God. Set on the Spirit: life and peace.
  3. Realm language matters — believers are in the realm of the Spirit if the Spirit of God lives in them; that is a finished position in Christ, not a feeling.
  4. Struggling ≠ lost — wrestling with sin in you differs from being wholly governed by the flesh without awareness (like fish that do not know they are wet).
  5. Our obligation — not to indulge the flesh, but by the Spirit to put to death the misdeeds of the body (Romans 8:12–13).
  6. Dedication and discipline — take the battle seriously; use spiritual disciplines as weapons that drive you to depend on God, matched to the root of your struggle (not mere rule-keeping that inflames the flesh).
  7. Resistance is evidence of freedom — the pain of fighting the flesh reminds you that you belong to the Spirit's realm; keep fighting.

🏛️ Caught Between Two Authorities?

Pastor Kevin opened with a playful story from university in Washington, D.C.: on Halloween, students would visit embassies — a kind of in-between space where earthly rules felt fuzzy. Spiritually, we are not meant to live in a grey zone: we are either under God's authority or under sin's. Yet many who love Jesus still feel like hypocrites — polished at church, but aware of hidden thoughts, anger at home, or obsessions no one sees.

The sermon asks: Is being governed by the Spirit all-or-nothing in experience? Is ongoing struggle normal? How do I know I am not in the wrong category? Romans 8:5–13 is Paul's answer.

🗺️ The Road to Romans 8

Pastor Kevin summarized Romans 1–8: one humanity condemned apart from faith (chs. 1–3); righteousness credited through belief, as with Abraham (ch. 4); peace, new life, release from the law's inability to fix the heart (chs. 5–7); then chapter 8 — no condemnation in Christ even while sin still lives in us. That tension is why we need verses 5–13.

🎯 What Is the Flesh?

📖 Romans 8:5 (NIV)
"Those who live according to the flesh have their minds set on what the flesh desires; but those who live in accordance with the Spirit have their minds set on what the Spirit desires."

Flesh here is a set of disordered desires — not only scandalous sins, but loves that are out of order (e.g. work over family) or disproportionate (something good becomes ultimate). The world's applause can mask fleshly fixation: career, children's success, fitness — good things that consume us.

The remedy Paul points toward is a mind set on what the Spirit desires — God's kingdom, justice, intimacy with Christ, growth in Christlikeness. Pastor Kevin illustrated disproportion with his own past obsession with World of Warcraft — thoughts consumed by the game, relationships diminished. Many know that pull in other forms.

⚡ Consequences: Death, Chaos, Hostility

📖 Romans 8:6–8 (NIV)
"The mind governed by the flesh is death, but the mind governed by the Spirit is life and peace. The mind governed by the flesh is hostile to God; it does not submit to God's law, nor can it do so. Those who are in the realm of the flesh cannot please God."

Death here is not mainly physical (everyone dies) but a deadening of spiritual sensitivity — less conviction, less joy in others' faith, more jadedness. The mind set on the flesh breeds chaos in relationships when self rules. Hostility to God means complete inability, on our own, to meet God's righteous demand — which drives us back to the gospel: righteousness is found in looking to Jesus.

Verse 8 raises a hard question about pleasing God and assurance. Pastor Kevin affirmed eternal security for those who trust Christ, and read "realm" language carefully: those who give lordship to disordered loves, who do not know they are ruled by them, stand in a different category than believers who struggle yet see progress over time.

🌊 Fish, Wi‑Fi, and the Realm of the Flesh

The "two fish" story: young fish ask "What's water?" — fish don't know they're wet. Living in the realm of the flesh is like that: total immersion without awareness. The Wi‑Fi analogy: modern life so thoroughly online that we barely notice dependence — a picture of how the flesh can quietly control everything. That is different from a believer who feels conflict and grieves sin.

🕊️ In the Realm of the Spirit

📖 Romans 8:9–11 (NIV)
"You, however, are not in the realm of the flesh but are in the realm of the Spirit, if indeed the Spirit of God lives in you. And if anyone does not have the Spirit of Christ, they do not belong to Christ. … And if the Spirit of him who raised Jesus from the dead is living in you, he who raised Christ from the dead will also give life to your mortal bodies because of his Spirit who lives in you."

If you have trusted Jesus, you are in the Spirit's realm — marked by the Spirit's indwelling. Pastor Kevin celebrated the gift of the Spirit as unparalleled intimacy with God now. Benefits include awakened sensitivity to God, transformed desires, and the promise of resurrection bodies — not disembodied clouds forever, but glorified physical life.

⚔️ Obligation: Put Sin to Death

📖 Romans 8:12–13 (NIV)
"Therefore, brothers and sisters, we have an obligation—but it is not to the flesh, to live according to it. For if you live according to the flesh, you will die; but if by the Spirit you put to death the misdeeds of the body, you will live."

We are not obligated to obey the flesh. "Die" here includes the deadening of spiritual life when we live according to the flesh. Our calling: by the Spirit, put to death the body's misdeeds — a real fight, with real weapons.

Two Ds: Dedication and Discipline

Dedication: name the flesh honestly; commit to long-haul battle. Discipline: Scripture, prayer, and other practices as tools that push us toward God — not to earn righteousness, but to attack the root of fleshly patterns.

Sabbath, busyness, and the Pharisee trap

Pastor Kevin shared struggling to keep Sabbath rooted in an addiction to busyness and a craving for validation. Surface fixes (stricter rules) could "succeed" yet inflate the flesh — the Pharisee dynamic. Disciplines must fit the diagnosed root (e.g. hidden service to starve validation) rather than legalistic performance.

Your resistance is proof of your freedom. You feeling the pain of your resistance against the flesh is a reminder that you belong to the realm of the spirit.

— Pastor Kevin (Romans 8 series)

The closing image: someone with no immune system feels nothing when ill — they just die. After a transplant, fever and ache are good news — the body can fight. Likewise, the struggle against sin is evidence that the Spirit's life is real. Battle on.

🌗 Two Realms: Struggling vs. Ruled

This tab gathers the sermon’s central distinction — the difference between sin still in you (which every Christian wrestles with) and the realm of the flesh (where disordered desires have total, often unrecognised, lordship).

🎭 Three Postures

😣
Struggling believer

You hate what you sometimes do; conviction returns; over time the flesh should lose ground. The fight is exhausting but real.

🌫️
Realm of the flesh

Disordered loves rule every area; like fish unaware of water, or life soaked in Wi‑Fi — the pattern is "just how things are." No pleasing God while Christ is rejected as Lord.

🕊️
Realm of the Spirit

If the Spirit of God lives in you, you are in this realm — positionally secure in Christ. Fruit includes renewed desires, conviction, joy in God's work, and hope of resurrection.

🔑 Key verse pairs

  • Romans 8:8 — "Those who are in the realm of the flesh cannot please God" — read alongside assurance texts; Paul is not denying eternal security but describing a posture of ultimate allegiance.
  • Romans 8:9 — "You … are in the realm of the Spirit" — the identity of everyone in whom God dwells by faith.
  • Romans 8:12–13 — obligation is not to the flesh; life comes through Spirit-enabled killing of sin's deeds.

🏛️ Why the embassy story matters

The opening illustration was not about minimising sin but exposing the myth of a comfortable middle: spiritually, we belong to God or to sin's dominion. Feeling "in between" emotionally is common; Paul's categories push us either to repentance and faith or to assurance and battle — not despair.

🎯 Governed by the Spirit This Week

Application flows from Romans 8:12–13: know your realm, name your flesh, and fight with Spirit-appropriate weapons.

🪞
Audit your loves

Ask: What is out of order? What has become ultimate? Bring one pattern to God honestly — the same questions the service reflection posed.

🎯
Diagnose root, not only fruit

Like the Sabbath example: surface behaviour may improve while the flesh grows prouder. Ask what validation, fear, or identity drive the sin.

🛠️
Match discipline to the root

Choose spiritual practices that starve the specific fleshly strategy (e.g. secrecy, humility, generosity) rather than generic rule-stacking.

🔥
Stay dedicated

Expect the same deep temptation to reappear in new forms. Commit to long obedience, not one-time fixes.

💪
Welcome the struggle

When resistance hurts, remember: that pain can signal the Spirit-given ability to fight. Battle on toward final victory in Christ.

Keep reading Romans 8

💭 Reflection & Discussion

📝 From the service

  • Is there anything in your life that is a misplaced priority? Is there anything you love with a disproportionate obsession?
  • What is one area of sin that you struggle with? What might be the underlying "fleshly problem," and how might you engage in a spiritual discipline to fight against it?

🔍 Going deeper

  • Where do you feel "between two authorities" spiritually? How does Romans 8:9 clarify your true position if you are in Christ?
  • Which consequence of a flesh-oriented mind (deadening, chaos, hostility to God's standard) shows up most in your relationships or inner life?
  • How can you tell the difference between conviction (Spirit) and accusation (enemy or shame) when you sin?
  • What would it look like to "put to death" one specific misdeed of the body this month with God's help?
  • Pastor Kevin closed with Ephesians 6:12. Who do you need to be praying for in the church's spiritual battle — including yourself?

🙏 Prayer prompts

  • For assurance: Holy Spirit, thank you that I am in your realm if I belong to Christ. When I feel like a hypocrite, remind me of the gospel and call me to honest repentance, not despair.
  • For discernment: Lord, show me the root desires beneath my visible sins. Give me courage to do the deep work of naming the flesh.
  • For discipline: Father, connect me to practices that make me depend on you — not on my performance — and that truly wound pride in me.
  • For perseverance: Jesus, when the fight feels long, let the pain of resistance remind me that you have already won the war and will finish what you started.
