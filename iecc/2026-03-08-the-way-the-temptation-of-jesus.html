📋 Sermon Overview

Central Theme: Our struggles against temptation are far more sophisticated than simply avoiding "big" sins. Jesus was tempted in all ways and was without sin. His three temptations in the wilderness reveal deeper questions every human wrestles with: Will God meet my needs? Is God powerful in my circumstances? Will I worship God only when there are rewards? Defeating temptation comes not from deeper resolve, but from fixing our eyes on Jesus and knowing who we are in Him.

Series Context: This is the second sermon in "The Way" — a Lent series exploring the life of Jesus as we make our way toward Easter. Jesus himself taught us to pray, "Lead us not into temptation" — and He can speak those words with authority because He endured temptation Himself.

🙏 "Lead Us Not Into Temptation"

The followers of Jesus came to Him with a simple question: "Teach us to pray." In response, Jesus gave them the Lord's Prayer — and within it, a line that is as direct as it gets: "Lead us not into temptation."

No interpretation needed. No original Greek to decode. The instruction is plain: pray that God would keep you away from temptation. Yet how often do we actually pray this? We tend to pray for provision, direction, answers — but rarely, "God, don't let me get close to temptation today."

The Paradox: Jesus teaches us to pray against being led into temptation — yet He Himself was led by the Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted. This is not something for us to model. Jesus is unique. Our role is to pray: "Keep me from it."

After fasting forty days and forty nights, Matthew records what may be the greatest understatement in the New Testament: "He was hungry." Every word of Scripture is there for a reason. This line isn't just about food — it reveals His state of vulnerability before the temptations came, and it confirms His full humanity.

⚖️ Fully God and Fully Man

Throughout church history, the nature of Jesus has been debated. Docetism said Jesus was fully God but not fully human. Arianism said He was fully man but not fully God. At the Council of Chalcedon in 451 AD, the church affirmed the hypostatic union: Jesus was fully God and fully man — both natures coexisting without diminishment.

Why does this matter? Because fully man means He understands us. He was depleted, vulnerable, hungry. He empathizes — not just sympathizes — with our weakness. And fully God means His sacrifice on the cross is sufficient to cover the sins of the entire world. Without both, the gospel doesn't hold.

📖 Hebrews 4:15-16 (NIV)
"For we do not have a high priest who is unable to empathize with our weaknesses, but we have one who has been tempted in every way, just as we are — yet he did not sin. Let us then approach God's throne of grace with confidence."
Because Jesus has endured temptation, we can come boldly before God — not with shame, but with confidence that He understands.

🏜️ The Three Temptations

In Matthew 4, the enemy comes to Jesus at His most depleted — forty days of fasting in the wilderness. Each temptation targets not just a surface-level desire, but a profound question of the soul.

1
🍞
Stones to Bread
"If you are the Son of God, tell these stones to become bread."
The real question: Will God meet my needs? Valid needs — not counterfeit, not unholy — but will He provide financially, emotionally, relationally?
2
🏛️
Throw Yourself Down
"If you are the Son of God, throw yourself down. For it is written: He will command his angels concerning you."
The real question: Is God powerful in my circumstances? Is He able and willing to enter into my situation, to see me, to care, and to respond?
3
👑
Bow Down for Kingdoms
"All this I will give you, if you will bow down and worship me."
The real question: Will you worship God only if there are rewards? Is your commitment contingent? Is there an asterisk by your faith?

🍞 Temptation 1: Will God Meet My Needs?

The enemy goes straight for Jesus' point of weakness — He's starving. "If you are the Son of God, meet a natural need in a supernatural way." This isn't about literal stone-to-bread power for us. It's the question bumbling about within your psyche every single day, whether you articulate it or not: Will God protect me? Will He provide? Will He answer my prayers?

Jesus responds with Scripture: "Man shall not live on bread alone, but on every word that comes from the mouth of God." He anchors His answer in truth that transcends the physical — there is something more sustaining than bread.

🏛️ Temptation 2: Is God Powerful in My Circumstances?

Having been defeated once, the enemy gets clever — he uses Scripture against Jesus, quoting God's own word: "He will command his angels concerning you." The devil says: Your God promised this. Test Him. Make Him prove it.

Jesus rightly applies the full counsel of Scripture: "It is also written: Do not put the Lord your God to the test." The enemy took a verse out of context; Jesus put it back.

The deeper question for us: Is God able and willing? When prayers go unanswered, when circumstances disappoint, the enemy whispers: "Your God doesn't care." But we can counter that whisper by recalling the ways God has intervened, answered, and provided in our past. He knows the number of hairs on your head. If a sparrow falls, He notices. If it's a concern of yours, it's a concern of His.

👑 Temptation 3: Will You Worship God Only If There Are Rewards?

The enemy introduces a transactional concept: If you do this, then you will get this. He shows Jesus all the kingdoms of the world and says: "Bow down and this is all yours."

Jesus responds: "Away from me, Satan! For it is written: Worship the Lord your God, and serve him only." He counters the offer of physical blessing with the reminder that true blessing is spiritual — to worship and serve God alone.

Is the quality of your life based on what is seen or what is unseen? We live in a material world. Hong Kong takes it to the next level. But purpose and meaning is found in recognizing that we are souls — there is an unseen side of life.

— Pastor Brett

📖 2 Corinthians 4:18 (NIV)
"So we fix our eyes not on what is seen, but on what is unseen, since what is seen is temporary, but what is unseen is eternal."
The gravitational pull of every life is toward the tangible, countable, material. Yet what is eternal is unseen — our connection with God, our identity as souls.

⚔️ How to Defeat Temptation

The temptation when talking about temptation is to think only about "big" sins. But the enemy has a plan for every stage, every age, every background — subtle strategies to distract you from devotion to God.

🔍
1. Know Your Weaknesses

In a world that covers up weakness, you might not even know your own. Restraint bias says you think you have greater restraint than you actually do. Scripture says the opposite: "Let anyone who thinks they stand take heed lest they fall" (1 Corinthians 10:12). Err on the side of stepping far back from temptation rather than snuggling up next to it.

💰
2. Imagine the Cost

If you thought about the actual cost of giving in, it would cut you short. Sin always brings death — death of trust, death of intimacy, death of relationships. "All at once he follows her... he does not know that it will cost him his life" (Proverbs 7:22-23). Sin never stays in a vacuum.

👤
3. Know Your Identity

At Jesus' baptism (Matthew 3:17), the Father declared: "This is my beloved Son, with whom I am well pleased." Immediately after, the enemy's first move was to attack that identity: "If you are the Son of God..." Jesus didn't need to prove it — the Father already said it. How much of our lives is spent proving things to people when God has already declared who we are?

The Skiing Story

Pastor Brett shared a personal illustration: at a party, three men were discussing skiing trips and fine wine — topics where he felt completely out of his depth. His temptation? To use words — sarcasm, passive-aggressive comments — to elevate himself at their expense. "I didn't go skiing. I was here doing ministry. But how are the Alps?"

Nobody was trying to make him feel small. But insecurity found the weakness, and his instinct was to use his greatest strength (words) as a weapon. If you don't know your weakness, you'll walk right into the temptation — and then wonder, "How did I get here?"

Church Hurt

Pastor Brett addressed "church hurt" directly: "If you stick around just a little bit, you're gonna be hurt — because somebody won't remember your name, or our worship will be too loud, or our aircon will be too low." The church is a group of forgiven sinners who try but still miss the mark.

But here's the plea: take that hurt to God, not away from God. Don't let horizontal disappointment become a conclusion about the vertical. God doesn't hurt you. God doesn't disappoint. And His greatest affection for you is proven once and for all at the cross.

📖 John 3:16 (NIV)
"For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life."
If you ever question God's love, look at the cross. He proved it, displayed it, once and for all. That proof can carry you through every disappointment.

✝️ The Greatest Defense

The greatest thing you can do in the struggle against any temptation is not to develop deeper resolve, some kind of perseverance, some determination that "never again." The greatest thing is to fix your eyes on Jesus.

It's not to avoid negative — not to say "don't sin, don't sin, don't sin" — but to love God, love God, love God. To understand how great His love is for you and to walk in that. It's not just to avoid temptation, but rather to be drawn into the beauty that is Jesus Christ.

— Pastor Brett

Yes, avoid temptations. But do so primarily by being swept up in how great God's love is for you. Worship Him alone. Serve Him alone. And watch how beautifully it affects your relationships with everyone around you.

🎯 Defeating Temptation

This sermon isn't just theology — it's a survival guide. The enemy has a plan for every stage of life, every age, every background. Here are three practical defenses, plus the ultimate strategy.

🔍
Know Your Weaknesses

Stop hiding behind a veneer of strength. Be radically honest about where you're vulnerable. What triggers insecurity in you? What do you reach for when you feel small, tired, or threatened? Write them down. Confide in a trusted friend. The areas you refuse to acknowledge are the areas the enemy will exploit.

💰
Imagine the Cost

Before you act, fast-forward the tape. Imagine the apology you'd have to make. The trust you'd break. The relationship you'd damage. Sin never stays in a vacuum — it always brings death in some form. Visualizing the cost is one of the most effective circuit breakers.

👤
Know Your Identity

So much temptation is really about trying to create or prove our identity. But God has already spoken. You are His son, His daughter. Christ is enough. You don't need to prove anything to anyone. Let the Father's declaration settle it.

✝️
Fix Your Eyes on Jesus

The greatest strategy isn't "don't sin, don't sin, don't sin" — it's "love God, love God, love God." Be so swept up in the beauty and love of Christ that temptation loses its grip. The vertical always transforms the horizontal.

📖
Arm Yourself with Scripture

Jesus countered every temptation with "It is written." Have God's word at your disposal — not just memorized, but internalized. When the enemy whispers a lie, answer it with truth. Scripture corrects what is erroneous and anchors what is true.

🤝
Bring the Hurt to God

When church hurts you — and it will — bring that hurt to God, not away from Him. Don't let horizontal disappointment become a verdict on the vertical. God doesn't disappoint. His love was proven at the cross.

🎯 This Week's Challenge

Identify one specific weakness — not a strength-disguised-as-a-weakness, but a real vulnerability. Write it down. Then pray the Lord's Prayer deliberately this week, pausing at "Lead us not into temptation." Ask God specifically to keep you from the situations where that weakness is exploited. And when you feel it pulling, counter it with one truth about who God says you are.

  • The enemy's first attack on Jesus was against His identity: "If you are the Son of God." Where in your life does the enemy most frequently challenge who God says you are?

🙏 Prayer Prompts

  • The Lord's Prayer: Pray Matthew 6:9-13 slowly and deliberately. Pause at each line. Especially linger on "Lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from the evil one."
  • Self-Awareness: Ask God to reveal your blind spots — the weaknesses you've covered up or the areas where you think you're stronger than you are.
  • Identity: Meditate on what God says about you: beloved, chosen, enough. Ask the Holy Spirit to make these truths louder than the enemy's "If you are..." whispers.
  • Healing: If you carry church hurt or disappointment with God, bring it honestly before Him. Ask Him to separate the horizontal from the vertical and to heal what needs healing.
  • Adoration: Spend time simply worshipping — not asking, not confessing — just telling God who He is and what He means to you. Let the beauty of Christ crowd out the pull of temptation.
