Central Message

Jesus makes the most exclusive and radical claim in all of religious history: "I am the way and the truth and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me" (John 14:6).

This statement is not arrogant presumption‚Äîit is truth backed by the resurrection. Jesus doesn't offer Himself as a part-time hobby or spiritual supplement. He claims to be the exclusive pathway to God, the embodiment of truth itself, and the source of all genuine life.

What troubles you right now? Jesus speaks directly into our anxieties, our burdens, our fears, saying: "Do not let your heart be troubled." We have responsibility for our emotional responses, and God equips us to face whatever troubles us through faith in Christ.

Main Sermon Content

What Troubles You?

‚ñº

Jesus begins with a question that cuts to the heart: What troubles you right now? Not theoretically, but in this very moment‚Äîwhat feels heavy, burdensome, draining?

He then gives a directive: "Do not let your heart be troubled" (John 14:1). The Greek grammar here is a present imperative passive‚Äîmeaning "stop being troubled." This places responsibility on us for our emotional responses.

Yet Jesus Himself was troubled. In John 13, knowing Judas would betray Him and Peter would deny Him, Scripture says "Jesus was troubled in spirit." So it's not about suppressing emotions or growing numb‚Äîit's about what we're troubled about and how we respond.

God never commands us to do something without equipping us for it. We are not innocent bystanders to emotional bursts. We have capacity and responsibility to respond appropriately through faith.

I AM the Way

‚ñº

When Jesus said "Where I am going, you cannot come," the disciples were confused. Thomas voiced what everyone was thinking: "Lord, we don't know where you're going, so how can we know the way?"

Jesus responds not with logistics, but with identity: "I am the way and the truth and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me" (John 14:6).

Jesus doesn't say the church is the way. He doesn't say having good theology is the way. Memorizing scripture is the way. Joining a care group is the way. All of these things are good, but these are a means to an end to help us encounter Jesus.

This is an exclusive claim‚Äîoffensive to modern ears. But it's backed by something unique: the resurrection. There are no bones of Jesus. No tomb where you can pay respects. He conquered death, which validates His claim to be the only way to God.

The early church wasn't called "Christians" at first (that came later as an insult). They were known as "the Way"‚Äîfollowers of the one who claimed to be the exclusive path to the Father.

I AM the Truth

‚ñº

Jesus said: "You will know the truth, and the truth will set you free" (John 8:32). The Jewish leaders were offended‚Äî"We're not slaves! We don't need freedom!"

But Jesus pushed back: "Everyone who sins is a slave to sin." Then He escalated: "You belong to your father, the devil, and you want to carry out your father's desires. He was a murderer from the beginning... he is a liar and the father of lies" (John 8:44).

There is a cosmic battle between truth and lies, light and darkness. God speaks truth. The enemy seeks to destroy through deception. We live on this battlefield daily, with both vying for our affection and attention.

Truth is liberating. It provides clarity, removes instability, gives confidence. When we know what Jesus says is true, we can bank our entire life on it.

I AM the Life

‚ñº

"For in him we live and move and have our being" (Acts 17:28). Jesus is not offering Himself as a part-time hobby to fit into your busy schedule. He's not interested in an hour and a half on Sunday only to be forgotten the other 142.5 hours of the week.

Jesus says: "I am life." And when you come to Him, He is the "transcendental interferer" (C.S. Lewis)‚ÄîHe will disrupt everything, change your affections, shift what brings you joy.

Christ, who is your life.

You cannot separate secular and sacred. There is no "God's area" and "my area." All of life‚Äîcareer, relationships, passions, hobbies, money, schedule‚Äîcomes under the beautiful umbrella of life in Christ.

It's scary to surrender completely, to not hold back certain areas. But it's only in that all-out surrender that we realize: it's so worth it. He gives purpose, meaning, hope, and contentment. In God's economy, nothing is wasted.

C.S. Lewis: Liar, Lunatic, or Lord?

‚ñº

C.S. Lewis, in his journey to faith detailed in Surprised by Joy, wrestled with Jesus's exclusive claims. He called Christ the "transcendental interferer."

What mattered most of all was my deep-seated hatred of authority, my monstrous individualism, my lawlessness. No word in my vocabulary expressed hatred than the word interference. But Christianity placed at the center what then seemed to me a transcendental interferer.

‚Äî C.S. Lewis, Surprised by Joy

Lewis wanted an area‚Äîhowever small‚Äîwhere he could post a sign: "No admittance. This is my business alone." But God would not stay out. Otherwise He's not Lord of all, just Lord of some.

Lewis famously presented three options for understanding Jesus's claims:

Liar

Jesus knew He was deceiving people but did it purposely with whimsical power.

Lunatic

He was crazy‚Äîdidn't know He was lying, just went around making false claims.

Lord

His outlandish claims are actually true‚Äîvalidated by His resurrection from the dead.

There are no bones of Jesus. No temple in Jerusalem where you can see His remains. If there were, we'd close the church. It would disprove everything. But the empty tomb backs up His claim: He is the way, the truth, and the life.

Modern Lies Versus Biblical Truth

Jesus said the enemy is the "father of lies" (John 8:44). We live on a battlefield where truth and deception compete for our hearts. Here are today's greatest lies exposed by Scripture:

‚ùå Independence is the ultimate pursuit

‚ñº

The Lie: Don't need anybody. Be financially independent. Work for yourself. Do what you want, when you want, how you want. Don't accommodate anyone.

The Truth: As parents, our goal isn't to make children independent from us‚Äîit's to transfer their dependence from us to God. To grow independent is to be stiff-necked, to resist authority, to resent submitting to anyone else's desires. This is anti-Christian.

"We are members of one body. When one suffers, we all suffer. When one rejoices, we all rejoice." We were created for interdependence, not independence.

‚ùå Vulnerability is weakness

‚ñº

The Lie: Don't let people see your weakness. Present an image. Manage your reputation. Show your wins, victories, strengths‚Äîthen pretend to be humble. But don't actually let people in.

The Truth: For the Christian, vulnerability IS strength. There's life in being honest, authentic, real. You can admit weakness because you're secure in your faith in Jesus Christ. You don't have to manage a reputation when God is your defender.

"My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness" (2 Corinthians 12:9).

‚ùå Sex can be casual or recreational

‚ñº

The Lie: "Friends with benefits." Sex is just physical recreation with no deeper implications.

The Truth: Sex designed by God is to be monogamous, soul-melting intimacy between two people in marriage. It is sacred, not casual. It always has spiritual and emotional implications.

"Flee from sexual immorality. All other sins a person commits are outside the body, but whoever sins sexually, sins against their own body" (1 Corinthians 6:18).

‚ùå Sabbath rest is outdated and unrealistic

‚ñº

The Lie: "I'm too important to take a day off. I'm just so busy." We wear busyness as a badge of honor.

The Truth: We should be embarrassed, even ashamed. God, the Creator of the universe, worked six days and rested one. When you say "I can't," you're saying you're more important than God. Don't be a fool. You need rest.

Sabbath is a display of faith‚Äîsaying "I can take a break for 24 hours and the world doesn't fall apart. I don't lose my job." If you have a crisis, please don't have it on Friday (Pastor Brett's day off)!

‚ùå Private sin isn't harmful

‚ñº

The Lie: If my sin only affects me, if nobody knows about it, it doesn't have a ripple effect.

The Truth: It ALWAYS has a ripple effect. You cannot sin in a vacuum. Secret sin shapes your character, hardens your heart, damages your relationships with God and others‚Äîeven when hidden.

"Nothing in all creation is hidden from God's sight. Everything is uncovered and laid bare before the eyes of him to whom we must give account" (Hebrews 4:13).

‚ùå Relationships can be formed efficiently

‚ñº

The Lie: We're Hong Kong‚Äîwe love efficiency! Let's do relationships faster. Schedule quality time. "Hey 13-year-old son, 6:30-7:00 tonight, let's have quality time where you unveil your soul to me."

The Truth: No 13-year-old will do that. You have to invest quantity time, and then you might get small nuggets of quality time. Marriage? You gotta spend time together‚Äîgood time, bad time, easy time, hard time, talk time, goofing-off time. TIME.

Relationships cannot be squeezed into an efficiency paradigm. Love takes time. Trust takes time. Intimacy takes time.

‚ùå Live your truth

‚ñº

The Lie: So popular. So many people say it. I kind of know what people mean...

The Truth: But listen‚Äîyou don't have a truth. There IS truth. And you should live according to truth, but you don't get to declare what is true. You don't get to "live your truth" unless it IS God's truth.

"I am the way and the TRUTH and the life" (John 14:6). Jesus doesn't say "live your truth"‚ÄîHe says "I AM the truth." Truth is objective, not subjective.

‚ùå All roads lead to God

‚ñº

The Lie: All religions are basically the same. All paths lead to the same mountaintop. It doesn't matter what you believe as long as you're sincere.

The Truth: That's really emotionally satisfying, isn't it? But God told us that's not how it works. Jesus is unique among all gods. Only He died and rose again. Only He conquered death.

Yes, all roads lead to the same mountaintop‚Äîbut not all faiths lead to the same God. "No one comes to the Father except through me" (John 14:6).

If it weren't necessary to go tell people about Jesus, we could save a lot of money and time. But we go because it's necessary. That's why missions matter.
