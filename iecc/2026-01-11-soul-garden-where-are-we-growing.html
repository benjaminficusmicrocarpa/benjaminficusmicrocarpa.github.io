The Crooked Forest: A Lesson in Growth

Pastor Tim shows a fascinating picture of a forest in Poland where trees grow crooked. The consensus is that man intervened and made these trees grow this way‚Äîperhaps for chairs or curved boat lumber.

üå≤ The Crooked Forest (Krzywy Las), Poland

Located near Gryfino in West Pomerania, this unique grove contains approximately 400 Scots pine trees planted around 1930. Each tree exhibits a distinctive 90-degree bend at its base, all curving northward before straightening upwards. While the exact cause remains a mystery, theories suggest human intervention to cultivate curved timber for boat building or furniture. The surrounding forest consists of straight pines, making this formation even more striking‚Äîa powerful visual reminder of how outside influences can shape our growth.
