Main Sermon

The God Who Sanctifies üî•

We're in this series called Known, based on the assumption that we all have an inadequate and incomplete understanding of who God is. No matter how long you may have followed after God, read his word, or been in Christian community, the reality is that you are limited, you are finite, and we follow an infinite God.

Throughout Scripture, God chose to reveal different aspects of His nature and character. There is not one name that could even come close to describing the totality of who He is. So God will choose a variety of different circumstances to reveal himself by changing his name.

What Does Sanctify Mean? ü§î

SanctifyTo set apart for a special purpose; to make holy. Related words: saint, holy, holiness literally means to set apart. To be different, to be noticeably different, to be observably unique. Biblically speaking, you and I as followers of Christ are set apart.

It's a two-fold understanding:

  • Negatively: Set apart FROM evil ‚Äî avoiding what is dark and anti-God
  • Positively: Set apart FOR God and His glory ‚Äî pursuing Him with new affections and priorities

The Context: Old Testament Sabbath

God's heart behind the Sabbath rules for Israel wasn't to impose legalistic expectations. By providing them a list of do's and don'ts, it would set them apart ‚Äî they would be counter-cultural. Their behavior would reflect their belief.

Note: Breaking Sabbath rules meant death in the Old Testament. Today, Jesus has fulfilled the law, so while we honor the Sabbath, the consequences are different.

Just as you can't pull yesterday's meal up to satisfy today's hunger, your soul needs daily renewal. God gives you what you need when you need it. This is how growth works ‚Äî daily surrender, daily grace, incremental change that compounds into transformation.

The Three Phases of Sanctification

Understanding how God transforms us: past, present, and future

PHASE 1

Past Sanctification ‚ú®

Positional | Instant

This happens in the moment you become a Christian. When you trust in Christ, you are transferred from the kingdom of darkness to the kingdom of light. Your eternity changes in the blinking of an eye.

1 Corinthians 6:11

"That is what some of you were. But you were washed, you were sanctified, you were justified in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ and by the Spirit of our God."

üí° Key Point:

You must understand WHO YOU ARE so that you know WHAT YOU ARE TO DO. Many people get this backwards, thinking they must do things to make God approve of them. But positional sanctification means God already sees you as a saint through Christ's righteousness.

PHASE 2

Present Sanctification üå±

Progressive | Gradual

This is the ongoing sense of growing in faith, maturing as a believer. It happens gradually as you walk with God, are around the things of God, and are changed by the Spirit of God.

2 Corinthians 3:18

"We all, who with unveiled faces contemplate the Lord's glory, are being transformed into his image with ever-increasing glory, which comes from the Lord, who is the Spirit."

üí° Key Point:

God loves you as you are, but He doesn't want you to stay that way. Wherever you are, He's got more. This is an active, proactive faith ‚Äî you cooperate with God's Spirit to see Christ-likeness formed in you.

"Let us purify ourselves from everything that contaminates body and spirit, perfecting holiness out of reverence for God." ‚Äî 2 Corinthians 7:1
PHASE 3

Future Sanctification üëë

Perfect | Eternal

This is what awaits all of us when Christ returns or we go to be with Him. Perfect sanctification ‚Äî the "already, but not yet" fulfilled. A day when all is healed, particularly within you.

Jude 1:24-25

"To him who is able to keep you from stumbling and to present you before his glorious presence without fault and with great joy... to the only God our Savior be glory, majesty, power and authority, through Jesus Christ our Lord, before all ages, now and forevermore!"

1 John 3:2

"Dear friends, now we are children of God, and what we will be has not yet been made known. But we know that when Christ appears, we shall be like him, for we shall see him as he is."

üí° Key Point:

Currently we see Him dimly with limited understanding. But we will have new capacities and purified hearts to comprehend the fullness of who God is ‚Äî without blemish, impurity, sickness, or tears. This is glorification.

Personal Reflection Questions üí≠

1. Where do you see change?

Can you look back and identify ways God has changed you? What affections, priorities, or behaviors are different now than before you knew Christ?

If you cannot see changes in your life, you have every reason to question whether you are truly a Christian, because change is normative in the Christian life.

2. Are you cooperating with God?

Sanctification is not passive. Are you actively engaging with God through prayer, Scripture, worship, and community? Are you choosing to walk by faith rather than sight?

Only God produces growth, but He does it when a Christian is activated ‚Äî when you move, believe, pray, trust, shift, change.

3. What are you investing in?

Your outer body is wasting away ‚Äî no amount of effort can prevent this. But your inner self is being renewed. Are you investing more in your soul than in temporary, external things?

"Why not invest in your soul? God tells you what wins in the end."

4. How do you respond to slow growth?

Are you frustrated that sanctification is gradual rather than instant? Do you understand that God gives daily grace for daily challenges?

Just as you need to eat daily, your soul needs daily renewal. God's method of gradual transformation keeps us in humble dependence on Him.
