📋 Sermon Overview

Central Theme: Being a child of God is not vague religious language or a temporary emotional state. In Romans 8:14-17, Paul describes a decisive adoption: by the Spirit, those who trust Christ are brought into God's family with assurance, freedom, intimacy, inheritance, and a new way to suffer.

Series context: Week 3 of seven in Romans 8. The series began with no condemnation in Christ, then life governed by the Spirit. This sermon shows what that Spirit-led life means relationally: believers are adopted children who cry, "Abba, Father."

🌟 Main Takeaways

  1. Adoption is a completed transaction — like a legal declaration that forever changes a child's name, belonging, rights, and future.
  2. Image of God and child of God are not identical — every person bears God's image and value; becoming a child of God is the gift given to those brought into Christ by faith.
  3. Spirit-led people fight sin — being led by the Spirit in Romans 8 and Galatians 5 is tied to resisting the flesh and confessing sin quickly.
  4. Children live in freedom, not fear — slavery fears the master, but children run to the Father.
  5. Adoption gives intimacy — the Spirit teaches believers to cry "Abba, Father" with childlike confidence.
  6. Children inherit with Christ — forgiveness, security, spiritual fruit and gifts, new nature, eternal hope, and access to God's riches.
  7. Children bring pain to God — suffering does not disprove sonship; it becomes a place to meet the Father who understands suffering.

👨‍👧‍👦 Adoption That Changes the Future

Pastor Brett began personally: all four of his children were adopted. He stressed that adoption was not "the easy way"; it required government approval, financial checks, medical reviews, criminal background checks, fingerprints, and finally a judge's declaration. Each time, the judge asked whether he and his wife willingly took full responsibility for the child with the full rights and responsibilities of a biological child.

When the gavel fell, the child's name and future changed. That is why Pastor Brett says his children were adopted, not that they are adopted as a lesser identity. The transaction happened in the past and permanently changed the future. Romans 8 uses adoption in a similar way: God has declared believers to be his children.

🪞 Image of God and Child of God

📖 Romans 8:14 (NIV)
"For those who are led by the Spirit of God are the children of God."

The phrase children of God can be used loosely, but Pastor Brett drew a biblical distinction. Every human being bears the imago Dei, the image of God, and therefore has equal inherent value regardless of age, gender, ability, capacity, or status. But not everyone is biblically described as a child of God.

In John 8, Jesus tells religious leaders that true children of God love the things of God and receive him. A child of God is someone transferred by faith from darkness into light, adopted into God's family through Christ.

⚔️ Led by the Spirit Means Fighting Sin

The sermon then asked: How do you know you are a child of God? Doubt, unanswered prayer, disappointment with God, spiritual dryness, and recurring sin can make believers feel wobbly. Romans 8 gives assurance, but not by pretending sin no longer matters.

Pastor Brett noted that "led by the Spirit" is used sparingly in the New Testament: Jesus led into the wilderness in Matthew 4, Paul's teaching in Galatians 5, and Romans 8. In each case, the phrase connects to the struggle against sin and the flesh. Spirit-led people are not sinless, but they do fight sin, recognize conviction, and confess easily.

📖 Galatians 5:16-18 (NIV)
"So I say, walk by the Spirit, and you will not gratify the desires of the flesh... But if you are led by the Spirit, you are not under the law."

🧼 Confession Rests on Christ's Finished Work

📖 1 John 1:9 (NIV)
"If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just and will forgive us our sins and purify us from all unrighteousness."

The word just matters. Pastor Brett pointed out that John could have said God is faithful and merciful, kind, or gracious. Those are true, but the text says faithful and just because Jesus has already paid for sin. If God withheld forgiveness from those in Christ, he would withhold what Christ purchased. Confession does not earn forgiveness; it receives and applies what Jesus has already accomplished.

🕊️ Freedom, Not Fear

📖 Romans 8:15 (NIV)
"The Spirit you received does not make you slaves, so that you live in fear again; rather, the Spirit you received brought about your adoption to sonship."

Pastor Brett used Juneteenth as a powerful illustration. Slavery legally ended in the United States on January 1, 1863, but enslaved people in Texas did not receive and experience that freedom until June 19, 1865. For a year and a half, they lived as slaves though legally free.

Paul's point is similar: do not live as slaves when Christ has made you children. Slaves fear the master; children run to the Father. Fear may be a default setting, but it is not the identity of God's adopted children.

🏠 Abba, Father

Adoption carried weight in the Roman world. Pastor Brett mentioned Caesar Augustus, adopted as Julius Caesar's heir. Adoption meant real authority, status, and inheritance. In Romans 8, adoption brings an even deeper gift: by the Spirit, believers cry out Abba, Father.

Abba carries childlike intimacy, like "Daddy." Spiritual disciplines such as worship, Scripture, Sabbath, fasting, and Christian community are valuable, but they are means to an end: growing in intimacy with the Father, not accumulating spiritual achievements.

📖 Hebrews 4:16 (NIV)
"Let us then approach God's throne of grace with confidence, so that we may receive mercy and find grace to help us in our time of need."

👑 Heirs Who Forget Their Benefits

📖 Romans 8:16-17 (NIV)
"The Spirit himself testifies with our spirit that we are God's children. Now if we are children, then we are heirs — heirs of God and co-heirs with Christ..."

The Spirit testifies within believers, corroborating the evidence that they belong to God. If we are children, Paul says, then we are heirs; and not only heirs, but co-heirs with Christ.

Pastor Brett illustrated this with an airport lounge benefit he had for years without knowing it. He lived "like a peasant" outside the lounge, paying for a small coffee and snack, unaware that access, food, comfort, and help were already his. Christians can live the same way: forgiven, secure, gifted, fruitful, and hopeful, yet unaware of the spiritual benefits already given in Christ.

🔥 Suffering With Purpose

Romans 8 does not sanitize life. Paul says heirs with Christ also share in his sufferings in order that they may share in his glory. Pastor Brett named the honesty of Scripture: life is hard, and for Paul it became harder after he followed Jesus.

But suffering should lead children to the Father, not away from him. God understands suffering from the inside. C. S. Lewis famously wrote that pain is God's megaphone to rouse a deaf world. Pain can stop us, realign priorities, and reveal what is real and true. The child of God can bring pain to the Father who sees and understands.

👨‍👧‍👦 Five Signs of Being a Child of God

This contextual tab gathers Pastor Brett's five diagnostic marks. They are not a ladder for earning adoption; they are signs of the Spirit's work in people already brought into God's family through Christ.

🧼
Confess Sin Easily

Children welcome loving conviction because forgiveness is already purchased by Christ. They stop denying, suppressing, excusing, or comparing sin.

🕊️
Live in Freedom

Fear is the old slavery. Adopted children run to the Father rather than living under paranoia, image management, or dread.

🏠
Value Intimacy

Spiritual disciplines are not trophies. Worship, Scripture, Sabbath, fasting, and community are paths toward deeper communion with God.

👑
Know the Benefits

Heirs of God should not live unaware of forgiveness, security, gifts, fruit, new nature, eternal confidence, and hope.

🔥
Bring Pain to God

Suffering does not have to drive children away. It can lead them toward the Father who understands pain and promises glory.

🧭 Diagnostic Questions

  • Confession: What is the last specific sin you confessed to God?
  • Freedom: Where are you legally free in Christ but still living as though enslaved?
  • Intimacy: Which discipline has become a checkbox instead of a meeting place with the Father?
  • Inheritance: Which benefit of being in Christ are you currently neglecting?
  • Suffering: What pain do you need to bring to God instead of interpreting it as abandonment?

🎯 Living as Adopted Children This Week

Application flows from the identity Paul gives: because you have received the Spirit of adoption, live like a child who belongs to the Father.

🧼
Practice Specific Confession

Name one actual sin before God without minimizing it. Receive forgiveness on the basis of Christ's finished work, then make any needed apology or repair.

🕊️
Challenge Fear Narratives

When fear begins replaying in your mind, answer it with Romans 8:15: the Spirit did not make you a slave again, but a child who can run to the Father.

📖
Use Disciplines for Intimacy

Choose one practice — Scripture, prayer, worship, Sabbath, fasting, or community — and approach it as time with your Father, not proof of spiritual performance.

🎁
Review the Benefit Package

Write out the benefits Pastor Brett listed: forgiveness, no shame, security in God's love, fruit and gifts of the Spirit, new nature, eternal security, and hope.

🤝
Serve From Your Gifts

If you do not know your spiritual gifts, take the church's spiritual gifts inventory or ask trusted believers where they see God using you.

🔥
Bring Pain Home

Name one disappointment, wound, or unanswered prayer honestly to God. Ask what it reveals, what it realigns, and how the Father meets you there.
