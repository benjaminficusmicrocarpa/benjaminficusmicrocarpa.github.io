üìñ Overview

Key Question: How do we fight against becoming spiritually lukewarm in our faith?

Central Message
The people of God can become spiritually stagnant, but we don't have to stay that way. By stepping out of our comfort zones, we can reignite the fire of our faith and become effective witnesses for Christ.

This sermon is the final message in the "People of God" series, exploring what it means to be authentic, loving, obedient followers of Christ. Pastor Kevin examines the church of Laodicea from Revelation 3:14-22 to understand spiritual stagnation and how to overcome it.

üèõÔ∏è Context: The Seven Churches of Revelation ‚ñº

The Apostle John received a prophetic vision of Jesus's second coming and circulated it to seven churches in Asia Minor (modern-day Turkey): Ephesus, Smyrna, Pergamum, Thyatira, Sardis, Philadelphia, and Laodicea.

These churches faced increasing persecution and needed hope. While Revelation's overarching message is hope, it begins with correction:

  • Ephesus: Loveless - "You have forsaken your first love"
  • Pergamum: Lax - Too compromising to worldly teachings
  • Thyatira: Liberal - Tolerating false teachers
  • Sardis: Lifeless - "You have a reputation for being alive, but you are dead"
  • Smyrna: ‚úÖ Loyal in persecution
  • Philadelphia: ‚úÖ Laboring church, working hard
  • Laodicea: Lukewarm - The harshest criticism with no affirmation
‚ùÑÔ∏èüî• Definition: What is Spiritual Stagnation? ‚ñº

The Geography Lesson: Laodicea sat between two sister cities in the Lycus River Valley:

  • Hierapolis: Known for hot springs and therapeutic waters
  • Colossae: Known for cold, refreshing spring water
  • Laodicea: Caught in the middle with lukewarm water

Hot water was useful. Cold water was useful. But lukewarm water? It makes you vomit. It's ineffective.

Spiritual Stagnation Defined
To be ineffective. No fruit from your life. You don't look different from the world. People aren't compelled to ask about your hope and joy. You're not performing acts of mercy or sharing the gospel with people you claim are important to you.

For a whole church: No life change happening. People aren't coming to faith, being baptized, or growing in discipleship. Just focused on internal infighting, never leaving the four walls to minister to the community. If the church shut its doors, would anyone even notice?

üí∞ Cause: The Three Idols of Laodicea ‚ñº

Laodicea was an economic powerhouse on a major trade route. After a massive earthquake in AD 60 leveled the entire city, they refused Roman aid and rebuilt with their own resources. Historian Tacitus noted they "recovered by its own resources without assistance from ourselves."

1. üíé Gold - Spiritual Poverty

Jesus says: "You say, 'I am rich; I have acquired wealth and do not need a thing.' But you do not realize that you are wretched, pitiful, poor, blind and naked. I counsel you to buy from me gold refined in the fire."

Laodicea worshiped wealth. But Jesus taught in Matthew 6: "Do not store up for yourselves treasures on earth... For where your treasure is, there your heart will be also. You cannot serve both God and money."

The question for us: How many of us derive self-worth from what we own? How many place financial gain over relational, personal, and spiritual well-being?

2. üëî Clothing - Spiritual Nakedness

Jesus says: "Buy from me... white clothes to wear, so you can cover your shameful nakedness."

Laodicea was famous for breeding Phrygian sheep that produced coveted glossy black wool. They clothed themselves with garments the world chased after, but spiritually, they were still naked.

Spiritual nakedness is shame from sin. Like Adam and Eve covering themselves with leaves after eating the forbidden fruit - we feel disgusted, unable to look people in the eyes after lying to loved ones, or so frustrated after relapsing in addiction that we hide from church and care group.

Jesus offers white robes of righteousness - the perfection God demands for relationship with Him. Through Christ's perfect life and death on the cross, this righteousness is given to us so we can have relationship with God the Father.

3. üëÅÔ∏è Eye Salve - Spiritual Blindness

Jesus says: "Put salve on your eyes, so you can see."

Laodicea had a medical school producing famous eye salve with anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. They were known for healing eyes, yet Jesus says they are blind.

Spiritual blindness is lack of understanding or discernment. Jesus continually criticized religious leaders and disciples for this - teaching lessons they failed to grasp.

The Greatest Indicator of Spiritual Blindness
When the truth of God doesn't translate into how we live:
  • We understand grace but remain condemning and judgmental
  • We understand forgiveness but harbor resentment and bitterness
  • We understand faith but run away when God asks us to do something hard
If you could truly see, you would live differently.

The Deeper Lesson

Everything Laodicea placed their confidence in became the source of their stagnation:

  • Banking ‚Üí Spiritually poor
  • Textile industry ‚Üí Spiritually naked
  • Ophthalmology ‚Üí Spiritually blind

The true cause of spiritual stagnation is our comfort zone.

üîë The Solution: Step Out of Your Comfort Zone

"Comfort always precedes stagnation."

When you first came to faith, you were excited, willing to take risks, jumping into things you shouldn't have, doing anything to serve God. But after several years, you settle into a comfort zone - only doing what's comfortable, only what you're good at.

The Comfort Zone Trap
  • Inner city ministry? "Not me, I don't speak Cantonese"
  • Prayer retreat on Saturday? "Not me, I'm more of a doer"
  • You only do what you're interested in, nothing else
  • And then in the blink of an eye: spiritual stagnation

üí° Think About Your Pivotal Moments

What do all your moments of spiritual growth have in common? You stepped out of your comfort zone:

  • Mission trips: Different culture, different country, doing something you never did before
  • Baptism: Got up in front of the church, shared your testimony, declared your faith publicly
  • Pastor Kevin's example: Leading his first Bible study despite never reading through a whole book of the Bible - God still used him

He's going to change your life. It will be completely disruptive. He'll take you out of your comfort zone time and time again.

But it's going to be so good.

And at the end of this long life of discomfort? "Sit with me on my throne."
