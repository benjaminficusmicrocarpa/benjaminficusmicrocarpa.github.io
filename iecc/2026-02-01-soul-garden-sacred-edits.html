üìä Four Layers of Sin

Based on the work of Robert Mulholland, Pastor Brett outlined four progressively deeper layers of sin that require increasingly careful self-examination.

1
Gross Sin

"Gross" as in big, obvious sins. These are what come to mind when we hear "sin"‚Äîsexual immorality, idolatry, sorcery, drunkenness. Usually the first things cleaned up when we come to faith.

2
Conscious Sin

Sins we're aware of but choose to do anyway. Often "respectable sins" that Christians tolerate: gossip, materialism, crude behavior, unforgiveness. We give each other a pass on these.

3
Unconscious Sin

Sins we may not be overtly aware of: wrong motives (doing right things for wrong reasons), sins of omission (not praying, not caring for the poor), dysfunctional relational patterns.

4
Attachments (Idols)

Good things made ultimate. Career, family, health, reputation‚Äîthings that aren't bad, but we've exalted them to be more important than God. If removed, our peace would be destroyed.

üò§ Example: Anger Through All Four Layers

Level 1: Gross Sin

Murder - The extreme version of anger. Obviously wrong. Don't do it.

Level 2: Conscious Sin

Yelling and name-calling - "You idiot! You stupid fool!" You have control, but you choose to express anger in harmful ways. You know it's wrong, but you do it anyway.

Level 3: Unconscious Sin

Passive-aggressive behavior, contempt, silent treatment - "What's wrong?" "Nothing, I'm fine." It's anger, but sophisticated and cloaked. Not overtly sinful, but punitive and undealt with.

Level 4: Attachments

Secondary discouragement - Example: Your daughter fails a test, and your own sense of well-being is disrupted. You've married your happiness to her performance. Her failure affects your peace because you've exalted this relationship beyond God.

The Litmus Test: If something was removed from your life, could you still be at peace? If any person, achievement, or possession must be present for you to have peace, that thing has become more important to you than God Himself.
