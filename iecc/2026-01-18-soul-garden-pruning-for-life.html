üå± Understanding Pruning

Pruning is the process by which you cut away dead or dying branches to make room for new and fruitful branches. But why do certain branches become less fruitful?

Plants need three things:

  • üíß Water
  • ‚òÄÔ∏è Sunlight
  • üå± Nutrients from the soil

When these resources are impeded or cut off, branches become less fruitful. Some examples:

  • Competition: Too many branches on one side competing for the same resources
  • Blocking light: Branches growing inward or crossing over, blocking sunlight
  • Disease: Infected branches that need removal to preserve the rest of the plant
  • Scarcity: During droughts, pruning redirects limited resources to the most productive branches
The Principle: Pruning redirects energy to make the plant fruitful. Similarly, God prunes us by removing even good things to redirect our energy, time, and focus to make us fruitful.

üçá What is the "Fruit" God Seeks?

In John 15, the primary fruit Jesus refers to is people won to Christ through evangelism and discipleship. The Greek word karpos refers to tangible, harvestable fruit‚Äînot just internal character qualities.

However, Hebrews 12 adds three more dimensions of fruitfulness:

  1. Holiness - Sharing in God's attributes, reflecting His otherworldly love and wisdom
  2. Righteousness - Living the life God calls us to, naturally and eagerly
  3. Peace - Being in right relationship with God, where everything is as it should be

üö´ Two Misconceptions About Discipline

Misconception #1: Every Difficulty is God's Discipline

The Truth: Not everything hard in life is God's discipline. Sometimes:

  • The world is simply broken (consequences of sin in general)
  • There's spiritual attack or oppression
  • It's just life being difficult

How to Discern: Look for the four specific results mentioned above. If a difficulty is producing those outcomes, it may be God's pruning.

Misconception #2: Discipline is Quick, Immediate, and Punitive

The Truth: Hebrews 12:11 says discipline produces fruit "later on" and for those who are "trained by it." This means:

  • There's a time lag - You might not see results immediately
  • It's repetitive - Training happens over time, not in a single event
  • It's formative, not punitive - God is shaping you, not just punishing you

üîó Family Systems Theory: "Cutoff"

In counseling, there's a concept called cutoff‚Äîwhen people try to heal by cutting out their past, thinking it's quick and painless. But:

Cutoff doesn't cut off anything‚Äîit seals your past. You don't eliminate your past; you freeze it. All your patterns of interacting, responding, and attaching are still drawn from your frozen past.

Example: If you were avoidant as a child because of tough parents, even in a loving community, you'll still be instinctively avoidant. Your subconscious patterns sabotage your best intentions to be vulnerable.

‚ùå Breaking Down Misconceptions

‚ùå Misconception #1: Every difficulty is God's discipline ‚ñº

Why This is Wrong:

Not every hard thing you experience is God disciplining you. Sometimes:

  • The world is broken - We live in a fallen world affected by sin
  • Spiritual attack - The enemy opposes God's people
  • Natural consequences - Choices have outcomes unrelated to divine discipline

How to Discern:

Look for these four specific results that come from God's discipline:

  1. Better Witness - Are you more effective in evangelism and discipleship?
  2. Holiness - Do you exhibit God's otherworldly attributes (love, wisdom, patience)?
  3. Righteousness - Do you naturally desire to live as God calls you to?
  4. Peace - Is your relationship with God being restored?
Practical Step: Engage in reflection to see if a difficulty is tied to sin in your life. For example: Were you fired because you were hard to work with, unapologetic, arrogant? Is that tied to pride lurking in your life?
‚ùå Misconception #2: Discipline is quick, immediate, and punitive ‚ñº

Why This is Wrong:

Hebrews 12:11 says discipline produces fruit "later on" for those who are "trained by it." This reveals two key truths:

1. There's a Time Lag

You might be going through something difficult right now, but you may not see the results immediately. You could be standing in the gap, and the fruit will come later.

2. It's Repetitive Training

Training isn't a single event‚Äîit's consistent, repetitive, and intentional. You don't run once and call it training. Similarly, spiritual discipline happens over and over.

We Don't Like This:

We want change to be immediate. We try to short-circuit the process by making transformation instant‚Äîcutting off all past relationships, starting fresh, thinking we're completely new. But this approach leaves many things undealt with.

The Pattern: God brings you to similar places and similar circumstances to give you opportunities to respond differently. This is how you truly heal and grow.
