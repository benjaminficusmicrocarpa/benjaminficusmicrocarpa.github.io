üåä The Woman at the Well: A Master Class in Evangelism

Pastor Brett unpacks John 4, where Jesus meets a Samaritan woman at Jacob's well at noon‚Äîa divine appointment orchestrated by God himself. Despite deep cultural antagonism between Jews and Samaritans, Jesus deliberately goes through Samaria.

What She Did:

  • Stereotyped: "You're a Jew and I'm a Samaritan woman. How can you ask me for a drink?"
  • Questioned: "Sir, you have nothing to draw with, and the well is deep. Where can you get this living water?"
  • Compared: "Are you greater than our father Jacob?"
  • Deflected: When Jesus revealed everything about her, she shifted to theology: "Where should we worship‚Äîthis mountain or Jerusalem?"

Her defenses were up. She had been hurt by men, rejected by society, and was carrying deep shame from five failed marriages and a current illicit relationship. Yet Jesus met her with something radically different.

What He Did:

Initiated Strategically

Jesus purposefully placed himself at that well at that time. He created the divine appointment, orchestrating circumstances to reach one woman who desperately needed hope.

Answered Respectfully

"If you knew the gift of God and who it is that asks you for a drink, you would have asked him and he would have given you living water." He heard her questions without condescension.

Taught Creatively

Using water‚Äîthe one thing they had in common‚ÄîJesus bridged the gap between cosmic truth and everyday life. "Everyone who drinks this water will be thirsty again, but whoever drinks the water I give them will never thirst."

Loved Radically

Despite knowing her entire sordid history, Jesus showed no judgment, no agenda, no exploitation. He offered honor, dignity, and transformative love.

üîÑ Her Transformation

The text tells us something extraordinary: "Then, leaving her water jar, the woman went back to the town..."

She came to the well at noon to avoid people because of her shame. She came for water. But after encountering Jesus, everything changed:

"She left the jar‚Äîit's no longer important. It's no longer her mission. She came at noon to avoid the women going as a group because she's an outcast. She doesn't have friends. She avoids people because her shame is great. She tries to be small. And then Jesus leaves her feeling big."

She went straight to the very people she had been avoiding and proclaimed: "Come, see a man who told me everything I ever did. Could this be the Messiah?" The very things she was hiding became her testimony. Her priorities completely shifted.
