üìñ The Message

Introduction: Good News They Couldn't Hear

Moses reported God's promise to the Israelites, but they did not listen to him because of their discouragement (Exodus 6:9). They couldn't even hear good news. Their hearts had become so discouraged that they could not receive relief.

This reminds us: discouragement can be destructive in your personal walk with God and in your relationships with others. If your heart has grown weary, if hope has faded, if your courage is lacking, that will impact those around you.

Paul's Credibility

The Apostle Paul gives us an incredible model for not losing heart. Consider what he endured:

  • Five times received 39 lashes
  • Three times beaten with rods
  • Three times shipwrecked
  • A night and day in the open sea
  • Often gone without sleep

Yet through all this, Paul writes in 2 Corinthians 4: "We do not lose heart." If he endured that without losing heart, perhaps our struggles aren't so insurmountable.

Relational Discouragement Is Not Inevitable

It's not God's design for relationships to become lifeless, for marriages to become mere roommate arrangements, for friendships to fade into polite distance. That kind of weighty relationship where you're not giving into or receiving from is:

  • Not healthy for anyone involved
  • Not sustainable over time
  • Not God-approved ‚Äî not His will for you

It is not God's will for you as a follower of Christ ‚Äî one who has greatly benefited from a God who pursued and loved you in spite of everything ‚Äî to turn to those around you and give up, to concede defeat, to give less than you could out of discouragement.

The Whole Chapter Devoted to This

2 Corinthians chapter 4 is entirely devoted to not losing heart. Paul begins: "We do not lose heart" (v.1) and concludes: "Therefore we do not lose heart" (v.16). Between these bookends, he details from his personal experience how he, of all people, doesn't lose heart.

The Warning

Galatians 6:9 warns us: "Let us not get tired of doing what is right. For after a while we will reap a harvest of blessing if we don't get discouraged and give up."

If you don't have a plan to fight discouragement, if you don't have an antidote for a world that gives you plenty of data to be discouraged, if you don't have a strategy for your own soul to fight through it, then you'll give up ‚Äî on people, on hope, on meaning.

‚ö° Sevenfold Strategy

From 2 Corinthians 4, Paul gives us seven practical ways to dismantle discouragement in our lives and relationships.

1

Make Much of God's Mercy

2 Corinthians 4:1

"Since through God's mercy we have this ministry, we do not lose heart."

Paul starts at the obvious: everything is by God's grace and mercy. He knew this well as one grateful he didn't receive the retribution he deserved. God spared him and gave him what he didn't earn.

You have received the same lavish, unearned, permanent acceptance from a holy God who enjoys you, delights over you, dances over you.

Application: Make much of God's mercy. Not just knowing it intellectually ("Yeah, I know God loves me"), but living in light of that truth. Exaggerate it. Focus on it. Fixate on it. Build it into your life.

üí° When you worship, are you thinking about the words? Are you singing them as your own prayer? When you taste something delicious (like pastor tacos!), do you see it as God's goodness?

2

Be Authentic

2 Corinthians 4:2

"Rather, we have renounced secret and shameful ways. We do not use deception, nor do we distort the word of God."

Paul commends himself ‚Äî not arrogantly, but authentically. "I present to you me. Warts and all. I am what I am. I don't try to be something I'm not."

The world has enough plastic. Metaphorically, there's too much fake. You get to be real. You're made in the image of God.

Application: Grow in your comfort level with who you are. Cut the umbilical cord of pretending, of managing your reputation, of being overly concerned about what others think. It's exhausting.

üí° If you're not comfortable with who you are, if you're not living authentically, you're providing the world more plastic. The church has the privilege of understanding we've been forgiven much and accepted in spite of who we are ‚Äî therefore we can be freed from being enslaved to others' opinions.

3

Combat the Tendency to Make Life About You

2 Corinthians 4:5

"For what we preach is not ourselves, but Jesus Christ as Lord, and ourselves as servants, for Jesus' sake."

Paul says, "This is not about me. My life's mission is that I would decrease and He would increase. That God would be made great. That my life would be lived for Jesus' sake."

Never has our world had such a focused, tailor-made, algorithmed strategy to make you think about you more. Your tendency is already to think about yourself enough.

Application: Have a plan to combat it. Walk into a room and think: Who can I bless? Who looks like they need someone to talk to? Who can I bring life to? Who can I compliment? Who can I encourage?

üí° One approach sucks life from a room; the other injects life into it. Who do you want to be?

4

Embrace Your Limitations

2 Corinthians 4:7

"But we have this treasure in jars of clay to show that this all-surpassing power is from God and not from us."

Paul calls us "jars of clay" ‚Äî clay pots that are easily broken and chipped. That's the fragility of our personal life. You and I have limitations.

Most of us spend so much life trying to hide our limitations, excuse them, improve upon them, or pretend they don't exist. But Paul says: You are a jar of clay.

2 Corinthians 12:10

"For the sake of Christ, then, I am content with weaknesses, insults, hardships, persecutions, calamities. For when I am weak, then I am strong."

Content with weakness. This doesn't mean you're not ambitious or that you don't try hard. But you recognize God has made you in a certain way. In Ephesians 4, it says He has "measured out, apportioned his grace to us."

üí° Spiritual maturity means not only being aware of your weaknesses, but seeing the redemptive beauty in them. "I'm never going to be Tim Keller. God's will is for me to be Brad Hillier. And God's will is for you to be you."

5

Leverage Your Pain for a Greater Good

2 Corinthians 4:8-9

"We are hard-pressed on every side, but not crushed; perplexed, but not in despair; persecuted, but not abandoned; struck down, but not destroyed."

Paul says, "All of this is for your benefit." When you encounter disappointment, heartache, struggle you didn't see coming, it's a secret of the Christian life to understand it ‚Äî not deny it, not over-spiritualize it ("Oh, but God is good!") ‚Äî but to look it square in the face and then say: God is good.

Application: We all have pain. You can either hide in it, sulk in it, circle the drain with it ‚Äî or you can process it over time and use it for something greater than yourself.

üí° Paul didn't just whine: "I was shipwrecked, beaten, lost..." He said, "All this happened for your good ‚Äî so you'd see the credibility of the gospel, that I didn't give up easy." The gospel gets credibility because Paul stood the course and endured.

6

Renew Yourself Daily

2 Corinthians 4:16

"Therefore we do not lose heart. Though outwardly we are wasting away, yet inwardly we are being renewed day by day."

Paul points out: You and I are souls. We are not bodies that contain souls ‚Äî we are souls. In the unseen world, the most important aspect of who we are (our soul) is being changed. While our body decays, our soul is being renewed.

But notice the frequency: day by day. Not week by week. Not just on Sundays. We need something grander than a Sunday opportunity to refresh our souls.

Application: Think about God daily. Pray daily. Recognize you are a soul daily. Cultivate patterns and habits ‚Äî reactions to the world ‚Äî that remind you of who you are.

üí° Make your faith pervasive. Look at the crowd on the MTR and remember 10% of people in Hong Kong know Christ. Spot nine people and think, They're real people. Maybe I should pray for them instead of doom scrolling.

7

Discipline Yourself to See the Unseen

2 Corinthians 4:17-18

"For our light and momentary troubles are achieving for us an eternal glory that far outweighs them all. So we fix our eyes not on what is seen, but on what is unseen, since what is seen is temporary, but what is unseen is eternal."

Recognize the reality that you are a soul. Know that prayer matters (you don't see it). Know that God speaks (even though you may not hear it audibly). The unseen world is as real as anything. Oxygen is unseen, and you believe in it.

Application: Believe in a greater unseen world where God dwells, moves, speaks, engages, delights, dances over you. Don't get sucked into the tangible only ‚Äî that leads to discouragement.

If your life is just deadlines, careers, paying bills ‚Äî it's meaningless, unless you see a God who is in all of it. But it requires discipline.

Questions to Ponder

1. Identifying Discouragement

What relationship (or relationships) in your life might be described as lifeless or in need of an injection of vitality, energy, thoughtfulness, and care?

  • Marriage?
  • Parent-child relationship?
  • Friendship?
  • Colleague relationship?

2. Signs of Relational Discouragement

Have you become:

  • Resigned? "I guess this is as good as it gets..."
  • Lethargic? Just phoning it in, lacking energy and care
  • Despondent? "It is what it is"

3. Making Much of God's Mercy

Do you truly live in light of God's lavish, unearned mercy? Or have you relegated "God loves me" to intellectual knowledge without daily experiential reality?

4. Authenticity Check

Are you comfortable with who you are? Or are you exhausting yourself by managing your reputation, pretending, and being enslaved to others' opinions?

5. Who Are You Living For?

When you walk into a room, do you think:

  • "Who's here? Am I dressed appropriately? What will I eat?" (Life about you)
  • "Who can I bless? Who needs encouragement? Who can I bring life to?" (Life for Jesus' sake)

6. Embracing Your Limitations

What weaknesses are you trying to hide, excuse, or improve upon instead of being content with how God has made you? Can you see the redemptive beauty in your limitations?

7. Leveraging Pain

What pain or struggle are you currently facing? Are you hiding in it and circling the drain ‚Äî or are you processing it and asking God how to use it for something greater than yourself?

8. Daily Renewal

What patterns and habits do you have for daily renewal?

  • Do you think about God daily?
  • Do you pray daily?
  • Do you recognize you are a soul daily?

9. Seeing the Unseen

Are you disciplining yourself to see the unseen world ‚Äî where God dwells, moves, speaks, delights, and dances over you? Or are you getting sucked into the tangible only (deadlines, careers, bills)?
