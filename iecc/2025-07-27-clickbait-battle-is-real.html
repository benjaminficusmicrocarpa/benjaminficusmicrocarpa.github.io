🌟 The Reality of Invisible Forces ▼

We readily accept the existence of oxygen, love, and other invisible realities based on their observable effects. Similarly, Scripture testifies to an invisible spiritual realm where forces of good and evil actively engage in warfare for human souls.

"The target is the church, not just this church, but Christians everywhere. For those who don't believe, they have no need to convince them of anything. But to those who have shifted their affections to God, then there is a battle that ensues to dissuade, discourage, to bring about doubt, temptation and accusation."
— Pastor Brett
Historical Church Response & Extremes to Avoid ▼

The early church (c. 200 AD) practiced a triple renunciation at baptism:

  • "Dost thou renounce Satan?" — I renounce him.
  • "And all his works?" — I renounce them.
  • "And all his pomps?" — I renounce them.
"Christians can get weird about this stuff. And I remember as a nonbeliever looking at churches who were casting out demons and people were falling down, I would be like, uh-uh, that's not for me. Is that part of this whole package? I'm not shaming those churches. I'm just saying scripture never gets weird about spiritual warfare. So don't be."
— Pastor Brett
"Oh, I was late paying my bill, Satan. No, you're just late paying your bill. That's irresponsible. I'm not Satan."

🛡️ Five Strategic Principles

"So five points for us as we wrap things up here... As you seek to take your faith seriously. It is not to fixate on the enemy. It is not to be overly concerned with spiritual warfare, to live in a sense of paranoia, seeking to assess blame to Satan and his cronies."

1️⃣ Take Warfare Seriously by Taking Faith Seriously

The primary defense against spiritual attack is spiritual maturity. Focus on knowing God rather than fixating on the enemy.

"It is rather to nurture your faith. It is to understand who God is, have your mind transformed, to replace those thoughts with new thoughts about the beauty of who Jesus is."
— Pastor Brett
The Sunday Challenge ▼
"Can I just say Sunday is not enough. You got to do more than Sunday. I try to pour this into your little mouths every week. Just force feed you. But you got to do more. Right? Because you're going to forget this sermon by Tuesday morning. Or Sunday dinner. It's so sad."
— Pastor Brett
  • Daily Scripture engagement beyond Sunday sermons
  • Regular worship that nurtures affection for God
  • Theological education to understand God's character
  • Community involvement to prevent isolation

2️⃣ Know Who the Real Enemy Is

Our struggle is not against human opposition but against organized spiritual forces led by Satan himself.

"You can see flesh and blood. That's easy to fight that battle. But Paul will say, no, it's against... And then he gives this organizational chart, if you will, of satanic forces."
— Pastor Brett
Enemy Organization & Intent ▼

Armor Pieces in Detail

🔗 Belt of Truth

Function: The foundation that holds all other armor in place

"You have to be able to distinguish lies. In my case that I was sharing, it was the staff doesn't support you. The elders are conniving against you. You're going to be asked to step down. They found somebody else. All this stuff is so ridiculous. Lies that were just whispers. But I gave them a moment's notice. And they took root within."
— Pastor Brett

🛡️ Breastplate of Righteousness

Function: Protects the heart from spiritual and emotional attack

"Doesn't your heart get weird? Don't your emotions confuse you at times? Don't they take you to places you don't want to be? Don't you feel things that you wish you sometimes didn't feel? He says you've got to protect your heart. You've got to make sure that your heart is nurtured for the things of God."
— Pastor Brett

👟 Gospel of Peace

Function: Readiness for spiritual deployment and service

"I'm not trying to guilt trip you into passing out a bulletin. Maybe you don't do it within the confines of the church. But you are called and expected to show up not as a consumer only, but one who gives your life away. To be used in your workplace, in your family. To be used in a beautiful way, spent for the glory of God."
— Pastor Brett

🛡️ Shield of Faith

Function: Defense against doubt and discouragement

"And the enemy would love to undermine that and give doubt. Do you really believe that? You're the only one in your family that believes that. Only 10% of Christians in Hong Kong. Can the 90% be wrong? Doubt. You have to combat it with the shield of faith."
— Pastor Brett

⛑️ Helmet of Salvation

Function: Mind protection from deceptive thoughts

"The helmet of salvation to protect your thoughts. That's what a helmet does. To make sure that your thoughts are filtered, are edited. That you have the ability to spew out what doesn't belong, what is not true. Because you know what lies are. You don't let them reside within you."
— Pastor Brett

⚔️ Sword of the Spirit

Function: Offensive weapon for spiritual battle

"You need to be as imperfect as it may be. You need to have God's concepts, his character, his words embedded within you so that you have something to stand on. Don't settle for a flimsy faith. Be scripture ready."
— Pastor Brett
