💬 What You'll Discover

Have you ever watched a romantic movie and felt something stir in your heart? 🎬 Pastor Eric uses this relatable experience to explore something much deeper - our universal longing to be truly "seen" and understood.

But here's the beautiful twist: the same principles that help us feel seen in human relationships can revolutionize how we see and experience God. You're about to discover a fresh, practical approach to spiritual intimacy that anyone can apply.

🛤️ The Emmaus Road Moment

Two disciples walked with Jesus for hours but didn't recognize Him. Then something changed everything...

"When he was at the table with them, he took bread, gave thanks, broke it and began to give it to them. Then their eyes were opened and they recognized him."

- Luke 24:30-31

💡 The question is: What needs to happen for your eyes to be opened?

🔗 Connecting the Dots: Movies → God

Step 1: Romantic movies make us feel something because they show people being truly "seen" and loved

Step 2: This feeling reveals a deep human need - we all want to be known and accepted

Step 3: Only God can fully meet this need because only He truly knows everything about us

Step 4: The same principles that help us feel "seen" by people can help us "see" God clearly

💡 The connection: Our longing to be seen points us toward the One who sees us perfectly - God.

📖 Key Scripture Passages

"See, I am doing a new thing! Now it springs up; do you not perceive it? I am making a way in the wilderness and streams in the wasteland."

- Isaiah 43:19 (NIV)

📍 Context:

God spoke this to His people during their exile in Babylon. They had been away from home for decades, and many were losing hope. But God wanted them to know - and He wants you to know - that He specializes in fresh starts.

💡 Personal Application:

The question "do you not perceive it?" is God's invitation to look up and see what He's doing in your life right now. What new thing might God be doing that you haven't noticed yet?

What this means in everyday words:

God is saying: "Look! I'm doing something new in your life. It's starting to happen right now. Can you see it? I'm making a road where there was no road before. I'm bringing fresh water to dry places."

💡 Like when you're lost and someone builds a bridge just for you, or when you're thirsty and someone brings you cold water.

"I keep asking that the God of our Lord Jesus Christ, the glorious Father, may give you the Spirit of wisdom and revelation, so that you may know him better. I pray that the eyes of your heart may be enlightened in order that you may know the hope to which he has called you..."

- Ephesians 1:17-18 (NIV)

This wasn't a one-time prayer - Paul says "I keep asking." Even mature believers need fresh revelation and clearer spiritual sight.

🔍 Notice:

The prayer is specifically for "the eyes of your heart" - not just intellectual understanding, but heart-level seeing and knowing.

"Yet I hold this against you: You have forsaken the love you had at first. Consider how far you have fallen! Repent and do the things you did at first."

- Revelation 2:4-5 (NIV)

Here's what's remarkable: The church in Ephesus was doing everything right - they were working hard, persevering, and protecting truth. But they had lost something essential.

⚠️ The Warning:

You can do all the "right" Christian things and still drift from the heart of relationship with God.

💚 The Invitation:

God isn't asking for perfection - He's asking you to come back to that place where your relationship with Him was fresh, alive, and central to everything else.

"For now we see only a reflection as in a mirror; then we shall see face to face. Now I know in part; then I shall know fully, even as I am fully known."

- 1 Corinthians 13:12 (NIV)

Paul acknowledges our current limitation - we see "only a reflection" like looking in an ancient bronze mirror. But notice what drives us forward: love.

🔗 The Connection:

This verse comes right after the famous "love chapter." Love isn't just a nice feeling - it's the lens through which we see God clearly, both now and eternally.

🎯 Four Steps to See God Clearly

💡 The Big Idea

What if seeing God clearly worked the same way as truly seeing someone you love? Pastor Eric shows us that the principles that create intimacy in human relationships can transform our relationship with God.

🧩 Breaking Down the Big Ideas

What does "feeling seen" mean?

Complex version: "Emotional validation and authentic recognition of one's inner experience."

Simple version: Someone understands your heart and accepts you as you are.

What are "assumptions"?

Complex version: "Preconceived cognitive frameworks that filter our interpretive lens."

Simple version: Ideas we already have about someone before we really listen to them.

What is "spiritual cataracts"?

Pastor Eric's metaphor: "We all have something like spiritual cataracts that keep us from seeing God clearly."

Simple explanation: Like cloudy glasses that make it hard to see clearly, sin and hurt make it hard to see God clearly.

🌏 Cultural Context for Hong Kong

Western vs Asian Relationships:

  • Western culture often emphasizes talking about feelings directly
  • Asian culture often shows care through actions and presence
  • Both approaches are valid ways to show love and be "seen"
  • God understands and values both cultural styles

💭 Remember: God created all cultures. He can "see" you whether you express yourself through words, actions, or quiet presence.

In counseling, there's a saying: "Don't work harder than the patient." The same applies to our relationship with God. If you're honest, do you really want to see Him clearly?

🤔 Honest Questions:

  • • Are you going through spiritual motions without heart engagement?
  • • Do you want God to be predictable and manageable?
  • • Are you afraid of what you might discover if you really "see" Him?

"For God, who said, 'Let light shine out of darkness,' made his light shine in our hearts to give us the light of the knowledge of God's glory displayed in the face of Christ."

- 2 Corinthians 4:6

✨ Good News:

If you're honest and realize you don't have the desire, that's actually a great place to start! The same God who said "let there be light" can give you new desires and a new heart.

🏠 Hong Kong Example:

Think about how you feel when you come home after a long day at work. Do you really want to spend time with your family, or are you just going through the motions? The same honest question applies to God: "Do I actually want to spend time with You, or am I just doing religious activities?"

In relationship counseling, this is where you put down your "weapons" - your history, hurt feelings, assumptions about motives. You show up ready to listen without defending or attacking.

🎒 What to Set Aside:

  • • Past hurts with church or Christians
  • • Assumptions about what God thinks of you
  • • Expectations about how God "should" act
  • • Your ideas about where your life should be right now

"God, I've heard things about you, I've thought things about you. I'm putting everything aside and showing up to you empty-handed. I'm willing to start new if I want to see you new."

- Pastor Eric's suggested prayer

💪 This Takes Courage

Setting aside assumptions doesn't mean pretending past hurts didn't happen. It means choosing to approach God without those hurts as your primary lens.

🏢 Workplace Example:

Imagine your boss calls you into their office. You might assume you're in trouble because of past experiences. But what if you walked in with no assumptions? You might discover they want to praise your work or offer you a promotion. Sometimes our past experiences with authority figures affect how we see God.

This is the simplest step to explain but often the hardest to do. You literally ask: "God, how do you want me to see you? What do you want me to know about you?"

🗣️ Simple Prayer:

"God, I'm listening. I want to know you in ways I haven't seen you before. I've put aside my assumptions. Please speak."

Then you actually be present. You don't fill the silence with more talking. You create space for God to reveal Himself through His Word, through prayer, through circumstances, through other people.

👂 Ways God Speaks:

  • • Through Scripture (His primary voice)
  • • In quiet moments of prayer
  • • Through circumstances and open doors
  • • Through wise, godly people
  • • Through His creation

⚠️ Important Note:

God will never contradict Scripture. If you think you're hearing something from God, test it against His Word and the counsel of mature believers.

👥 Family Example:

When someone you love is upset, the best thing you can do is ask: "What's really bothering you? Help me understand." Then you listen without trying to fix everything immediately. God wants us to approach Him the same way: "God, help me understand who You really are."

This is where the rubber meets the road. In a human relationship, trust means believing what someone tells you about themselves and responding accordingly. The same applies with God.

🚀 What This Looks Like:

  • • If God says He loves you, stop acting like He's angry with you
  • • If God says He forgives you, stop punishing yourself
  • • If God says He has plans for you, stop acting hopeless
  • • If God says He's always with you, stop living in fear

This doesn't mean blind faith - it means informed trust. God has proven His character through thousands of years of faithful dealing with His people.

"Trust in the LORD with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight."

- Proverbs 3:5-6

⭐ The Beautiful Result:

When you consistently approach God with love - checking your heart, setting aside assumptions, listening, and trusting - you'll experience the fresh start He's been offering all along.

🚇 MTR Example:

When you tap your Octopus card at the MTR gate, you trust it will open - even though you can't see your balance. You act based on what the system tells you is true. Faith works similarly: when God tells you something about Himself, you can act on it even when you can't see everything clearly.

Sometimes it's easier to practice these principles where we can see immediate feedback. As you experience breakthrough in human relationships, you'll better understand how to approach God with the same intentionality.

🧠 Understanding Counseling Terms in Simple Language

"Don't work harder than the patient"

Counseling meaning: Don't try to help someone more than they want to be helped.

God application: God won't force you to have a relationship with Him. He waits for you to want it too.

"Active listening"

Counseling meaning: Giving someone your full attention without planning what to say next.

God application: When we pray, we should also spend time quietly listening for God's response.

"Put down your weapons"

Counseling meaning: Stop using past hurts to attack or defend in conversations.

God application: Come to God without using past disappointments as reasons why He can't be trusted.

"Trust what they say they see"

Counseling meaning: Believe someone when they tell you how they feel or what they experienced.

God application: When God tells you who He is through Scripture, believe Him rather than your feelings.

🚧 Common Obstacles (And How to Overcome Them)

"I don't hear God speaking"

Remember: God's primary way of speaking is through Scripture. Start there. Also, His voice is often quieter than you expect - more like a gentle impression than an audible voice.

💡 Try this: Read one Psalm each morning and ask, "God, what do you want me to see about you here?"

"I can't set aside my hurt/disappointment"

Don't try to pretend the hurt isn't real. Instead, acknowledge it, then choose to approach God despite it. Say: "God, I'm hurt, but I'm willing to see if there's more to the story."

💡 Remember: Setting aside assumptions doesn't mean ignoring your feelings - it means not letting them control the conversation.

"This feels too emotional/touchy-feely"

That's okay! Faith doesn't require you to be emotionally expressive. The goal isn't feelings - it's seeing God as He truly is, which is actually very practical.

💡 Focus on: "What does God want me to know?" rather than "What should I feel?"
