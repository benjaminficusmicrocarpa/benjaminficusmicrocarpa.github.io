üëπ Demonic Manifestation vs. Invisible Warfare

+

Pastor Kevin Kusunoki explains the spectrum of spiritual warfare:

Demonic Oppression üë§

External pressure from demonic forces attempting to influence, torment, tempt, and discourage. The person retains full control but faces immense spiritual pressure.

Demonic Possession üòà

Internal control where a person loses complete control of themselves and their faculties. Very rare in Scripture (e.g., the demoniac possessed by Legion).

‚úùÔ∏è Can Christians Be Possessed?

No. First Corinthians 6:19 declares that our bodies are temples of the Holy Spirit. Demons cannot exert full control over a temple of God.

However, Christians can give the devil a "foothold" through unrepentant sin (Ephesians 4:26-27). This foothold allows intensified spiritual oppression that can feel overwhelming, but it is not possession.

üçé Genesis 3: The Original Deception Strategy

+

God's Original Command (Genesis 2:16-17)

"You are free to eat from any tree in the garden, but you must not eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, for when you eat from it you will certainly die."

Notice: God's command emphasized freedom and generosity with only one restriction for their protection.

Satan's First Move: Distortion (Genesis 3:1)

"Did God really say, 'You must not eat from any tree in the garden'?"

The tactic: Satan completely reverses God's generous statement, making God appear harsh and restrictive. This blatant lie serves a purpose: to engage Eve in dialogue and make her feel it's safe to converse with the enemy.

Eve's Response: Correction but Compromise

"We may eat fruit from the trees in the garden, but God did say, 'You must not eat fruit from the tree that is in the middle of the garden, and you must not touch it, or you will die.'"

Eve tries to correct Satan but adds "you must not touch it" ‚Äî something God never said. By engaging in debate, Eve gave Satan a foothold into her life.

Satan's Second Move: Contradiction (Genesis 3:4-5)

"You will not certainly die. For God knows that when you eat from it your eyes will be opened, and you will be like God, knowing good and evil."

Satan's Three-Pronged Attack:
  • Contradicts God's word: "You will not die"
  • Questions God's goodness: "God is withholding something good from you"
  • Positions himself as beneficial: "I'm telling you the truth for your benefit"

The Result: Sin and Blame-Shifting

Eve ate and gave fruit to Adam. When confronted by God, Adam's response reveals the corruption of sin:

"The woman you put here with me‚Äîshe gave me some fruit from the tree, and I ate it."

Adam blamed both Eve and God. Blame-shifting, especially blaming God when things go wrong, is the act of Satan.

‚ö° Ignatius of Loyola: Discernment of Spirits

+

Ignatius of Loyola, founder of the Jesuits, developed 14 rules for "Discernment of Spirits" in his Spiritual Exercises. Pastor Albert highlights the first two:

Rule 1: For the Ungodly

Target: "Persons who are going from mortal sin to mortal sin" ‚Äî those deliberately turning from God and knowingly living in sin.

Satan's tactic: Proposes apparent pleasures, leading them to imagine sensual delights and pleasures.

The Addiction Cycle:

Satan leads you to imagine satisfaction, but the reality never matches the promise. You're left wanting more but never satisfied ‚Äî hooked like a fish in an endless cycle of over-promise and under-delivery.

The Holy Spirit's response: Stings and bites their conscience through moral judgment, convicting them and pulling them back toward God.

Rule 2: For the Godly

Target: "Persons who are going on intensely purifying their sins and rising from good to better" ‚Äî believers striving to honor God.

Satan's tactic: Bites, sets obstacles, and disquiets with false reasons to prevent forward movement toward God.

The Goal:

Discouragement. Satan attacks your heart with obstacles and your mind with false reasoning to keep you from drawing closer to God.

The Holy Spirit's response: Gives courage, strength, consolation, tears, quiet, easing obstacles, and enabling you to go forward in doing good.

The Goal:

Encouragement. The Holy Spirit encourages your heart and gives clarity of mind to move you closer to God.
