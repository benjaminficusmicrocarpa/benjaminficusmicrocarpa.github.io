üå≥ Message 1: The Blessing of Psalm 1 (Zelda)

The Chinese New Year Context

During Chinese New Year, we exchange blessings like "Gong Hei Fat Choi" (wishing prosperity) or "San Tai Kin Hong" (wishing good health). But the Bible offers blessings with instructions on how to obtain them.

The Structure of Psalm 1:1-3

  • Verse 1: What NOT to do (avoid the wicked, sinners, mockers)
  • Verse 2: What TO do (delight in God's law, meditate day and night)
  • Verse 3: The BLESSING (like a tree by streams of water)

The Promise: Like a Tree by Water üå≥

The blessed person is "like a tree planted by streams of water, which yields its fruit in season and whose leaf does not wither. Whatever they do prospers." This imagery shows peace, strength, unlimited nourishment, fruitfulness, and success.

Who to Avoid

Three groups represent the anti-God camp:

  • The Wicked: Hostile to God and His people
  • The Sinners: In open rebellion, habitual offenders
  • The Mockers: Refuse to learn God's way and ridicule those who do

Notice the progression: walking ‚Üí standing ‚Üí sitting. Sin tightens its grip gradually when we're not careful about our influences.

What to Do Instead

Delight in the law of the Lord and meditate on it day and night.

Key insights:

  • "Meditate" means reciting under your breath‚Äîfull attention, not just thinking
  • "Delight" means longing and desire‚Äîdo it joyfully, not as a checklist
  • "Day and night" means continually, all the time
Zelda's Key Point: "We are influenced by what we surround ourselves with. Changes happen gradually, subtly, and often subconsciously. That's why surrounding ourselves with God's Word keeps us close to the source of every blessing."

Two Foundational Truths

1. We are influenced by what we surround ourselves with
Just as new Hong Kong residents eventually walk as fast as locals, we become like those around us. Surround yourself with God's Word to become more like Him.

2. God is the source of every blessing
James 1:17 tells us: "Every good and perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of the Heavenly Lights." Behind every blessing is one wellspring‚Äîour Heavenly Father.

The "In Season" Reminder

Even the blessed tree only yields fruit "in season." We all have fruitful and less fruitful seasons. But God's promise is that when you stay close to Him, you remain nourished, at peace, and growing‚Äîso when it's your season, you'll bear beautiful fruit and prosper.

Point 1: Don't Underestimate Health ‚öïÔ∏è

Health is holistic: body, mind, soul, spirit, and community.

The WHO defines health as "a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being‚Äînot merely the absence of disease." The Bible has always taught this.

Physical + Mental Health

Proverbs 17:22: "A joyful heart is good medicine, but a crushed spirit dries up the bones."

Chronic stress, sadness, anger, and anxiety impact our immune system, leading to heart disease and high blood pressure. We must guard our mental health diligently.

Social Health

The Harvard Study of Adult Development (85+ years) found: Positive relationships keep us happier, healthier, and help us live longer.

Genesis 2:18: "It is not good for the man to be alone." We are created for connection. Loneliness is as harmful as smoking or alcoholism.

Spiritual Health

We are fundamentally spiritual beings. We cannot be truly healthy if our spirit is sick due to separation from God.

1 Timothy 4:8: "Bodily exercise profits a little, but godliness is profitable for all things, having promise of the life that now is and of that which is to come."

Spiritual exercise (prayer, Scripture meditation, community) nourishes us at the deepest level.

Point 2: Don't Overestimate Health ‚öñÔ∏è

Health matters, but it's not ultimate.

"Which of you by being anxious can add a single hour to his span of life?" (Matthew 6:27)

If we overestimate health:

  • We become obsessed, constantly worried, chasing quick fixes
  • We idolize it, making it our ultimate concern (e.g., "We Go Gym" trend)
  • We overestimate controllability‚Äîbut even the fittest can face illness

Hong Kongers spent HK$18.5 billion on health products in six months‚Äîsome beneficial, some driven by panic. We must put health in perspective.

Resurrection hope gives us courage to face illness, aging, and suffering without losing joy. It frees us from health anxiety and fear of death.
