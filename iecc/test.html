Biblical Foundation: Not of This World

Christ's Prayer for Believers

"I have given them your word, and the world has hated them because they are not of the world, just as I am not of the world. I do not ask that you take them out of the world, but that you keep them from the evil one. They are not of this world, just as I am not of the world. Sanctify them in the truth; your word is truth. As you sent me into the world, so I have sent them into the world."

🎯 Westminster Catechism

"What is the chief end of man? Man's chief end is to glorify God and to enjoy him forever."

— Westminster Shorter Catechism (1647)

This foundational question establishes our primary purpose across every season of life. Whether single, married, or parenting, our ultimate aim remains constant: to glorify GodLiving in a way that demonstrates God's character and brings Him honor and find our deepest satisfaction in Him.

📚 Purpose-Driven Life

"Without God, life has no purpose. And without purpose, life has no meaning. Without meaning, life has no significance or hope."

— Rick Warren, The Purpose-Driven Life

Warren's insight reveals why secular approaches to life phases often leave us empty. True fulfillment emerges not from achieving the next milestone, but from understanding our divine purpose in each present moment.

The Eternal Perspective

Paul's declaration, "to live is Christ" (Philippians 1:21), transcends every cultural expectation and life transition. This perspective transforms how we approach:

  • • Singleness from independence-seeking to God-dependence
  • • Marriage from personal happiness to mutual sanctification
  • • Parenting from child-centered to God-centered family life

The Nature of Covenant Love

"Love, as distinct from being in love, is not merely a feeling. It is a deep unity maintained by the will and deliberately strengthened by habit. Reinforced by the grace which both partners ask and receive from God."

— C.S. Lewis, Mere Christianity

Lewis distinguished between the euphoric explosion of being in loveThe initial romantic feelings that spark a relationship and the deliberate engine of covenant loveThe deliberate choice to serve and sacrifice for one's spouse that sustains marriage through every season.

The Great Commission of the Home

"Train up a child in the way he should go; even when he is old he will not depart from it."

"You shall teach them diligently to your children, and shall talk of them when you sit in your house, and when you walk by the way, and when you lie down, and when you rise."

Children as Disciples

The profound realization emerges: our children become our primary disciplesPeople we are intentionally teaching and forming in the Christian faith. We serve as their first pastors, their initial theological teachers, their foundational models of what it means to follow Christ.

Note the intentional language: we must train and teach diligently. This divine calling demands focused effort, consistent presence, and unwavering commitment to spiritual formation.

Practical Wisdom for Faithful Living

The Unifying Principle

"Whether you eat or drink, or whatever you do, do it all for the glory of God."

This divine mandate transcends every life phase, every relationship status, every family structure. Our calling remains constant: to live as lights in the world, demonstrating God's character through our choices, relationships, and daily rhythms.

  • Serve together as a unified team in God's kingdom
  • Model Christ's love for the church through your relationship

👶 If You're Parenting

  • Create a loving, safe environment that reflects God's character
  • Disciple your children through diligent teachingConsistent, intentional teaching moments throughout daily life
  • Model authentic faith through your actions and choices
  • Invest time as the primary expression of love
