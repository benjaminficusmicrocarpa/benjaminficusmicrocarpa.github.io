Sermon Overview 🎯

Our days are often full of worry. We fall into a routine of trying to control everything—our schedules, our work, our relationships—doing our best to hold it all together. And it's exhausting. We need a Fresh Start. We need a way to step out of the cycle, loosen our grip, and trust God more deeply with our lives. Today, we're going to explore practical ways to grow in faith and experience the peace God promises, even in the midst of life's chaos.

Main Sermon Content 📖

Pastor Tim opened by highlighting how trust is something we deal with every single day. Do we trust the weather forecast? Our bus drivers? The barber with our hair? Our phones to last all day? We put trust in countless things, yet we're constantly being deceived.

Examples of modern deception include spam calls starting with "3", phishing emails, and increasingly sophisticated AI-generated content that makes it harder than ever to distinguish truth from falsehood. This constant deception conditions us toward mistrust.

The sermon posed the critical question: In a world where we can't trust anything, who can we trust? What can we trust?

Moses' Incredible Track Record:

  • ✓ Confronting Pharaoh: Faced one of the most powerful men in the world, trusting God's protection
  • ✓ Parting the Red Sea: Stretched out his hand and the sea parted, saving the Israelites from Pharaoh's army
  • ✓ Provision of Manna: Trusted God to rain down bread from heaven for sustenance in the wilderness
  • ✓ Water from the Rock (First Time): Struck the rock at Horeb and water came out
  • ✓ Victory over the Amalekites: Held up his staff for victory in battle
  • ✓ Meeting God at Mount Sinai: Received the Ten Commandments

The Fatal Mistake:

After 40 years of faithful leadership, the Israelites were thirsty again. This time, God told Moses to speak to the rock, not strike it. But Moses, frustrated with the people, called them "rebels" and said "Must WE bring you water?" (taking credit for God's work) and struck the rock twice instead of speaking to it.

This illustrates the profound truth: "Trust takes years to build, seconds to break, and forever to repair."

1. We Like Control 🎮

We have our schedules, diaries, planners, budgets, banking apps, fitness trackers—we try to maintain complete control of everything. We predict our own future. But eventually chaos erupts (tragedy, health concern, financial crisis) and throws us off balance. That's usually when people come running back to God.

Antidote: God is in Command

2. We're Scared 😰

We're scared of an unknown future, of taking risks, of taking a leap of faith. We don't want to rock the boat or change the status quo. Maybe God is calling us to something right now but we're too scared to take the jump, even though God has something great in store.

Antidote: God is Our Courage

3. We've Been Disappointed 💔

We've been disappointed by other people, so we don't trust them as much. But we can also be disappointed by God—maybe we've been praying about something for so long, giving it to God, but He's not coming through for us or not answering the prayer in the way we want.

Antidote: God Never Fails

4. We Don't Understand God's Ways 🤔

When we buy something or invest, we do all the research first to understand if it's a good investment. We're meticulous about understanding details before jumping in. But God says to flip that around: Believe in me, trust in me first, and then grow in your understanding. We struggle with this.

Antidote: God is Wise

5. We Want Instant Gratification ⚡

We want things to happen right now. We treat God like a vending machine—push a button and the thing happens. But God wants us to be patient and work in His time, not ours. The Israelites' 11-day journey took 40 years because God was refining them to be His holy nation.

Antidote: God's Timing is Perfect

The Parent-Child Parallel 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Pastor Tim shared how having children helps us understand our relationship with God. Kids have no control but act like they do, want instant gratification, don't understand our bigger plans (like waiting for ice cream after dinner or waiting until they're old enough for a dog). When we act the same way toward God, He's looking at us thinking, "What are you doing? I have a much bigger, better plan for you."

We often limit God because we don't believe He can do everything we're asking. In Mark 9, a father brought his son (who had an impure spirit causing convulsions and inability to speak) to Jesus and said: "But if you can do anything, have compassion on us and help us."

Jesus' Response:

"'If you can'?!"

The exclamation point shows Jesus was animated—"Of course! Who are you talking to right now? All things are possible for one who believes."

The Perfect Human Response 🙏

Immediately the father cried out:

"I believe; help my unbelief!"

This is all of us. We believe, but we don't always believe. We go through disappointments, periods of doubt, struggle with lack of hope. If we would just cry out, "God, I believe in you, help my unbelief," that would be incredibly helpful.

Pastor Tim shared a personal story from 2006 when his family lived on Lamma Island. One windy night, they missed the last ferry to their village and had to take a sampan across the Lamma Channel (South China Sea).

The Experience:

  • The boat rocked violently in the wind
  • Container ships passing by created massive waves
  • Pastor Tim was holding one-year-old Lily, absolutely terrified
  • He and Cindy were making emergency plans, joking about Titanic scenarios
  • He was questioning all his life decisions

The Turning Point 🌊

In the midst of all this chaos, Pastor Tim looked at the boat captain—who was completely stoic, holding the motor, looking like they were on crystal clear waters. Not a care in the world.

"All of a sudden, this peace which surpassed all understanding came upon me. I thought, 'Oh, okay. He looks like he's under control. If he's doing fine, then I think we'll be okay.'"

The Parallel: This is exactly how we should look to God in our storms. If we would just look to Him, we can find that transcendent peace even in chaos. Like Jesus sleeping in the boat during the storm while the disciples panicked—He asked them, "Why are you so afraid? Do you still have no faith?"

Philippians 4:6-7 (NIV)

"Do not be anxious about anything, but in every situation, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God. And the peace of God, which transcends all understanding, will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus."

1. Prayer 🙏

Acknowledge God and His goodness.

Fresh Start Practice:

Every morning, give the day to God and start it with prayer. Before getting out of bed or looking at your phone, thank God and praise Him that you have life, that He is who He is, that He loves us and has a plan for us.

2. Petition 📝

Bring your specific need or worry. Give that to God.

Fresh Start Practice:

Keep a prayer journal (on paper or your phone) so you can see how God answers those prayers. Set a daily notification to write down your prayer requests.

3. Thanksgiving 🙌

Thank Him for hearing you and for His work.

Fresh Start Practice:

Record your gratitude. Think about and write down the things you're thankful for every single day. Don't take anything for granted—the air in your lungs, roof over your head, food, job, friends, family.

4. Release 🎈

Hand it over and trust Him.

Fresh Start Practice:

Pause in your day and make declarations. Set an alarm or when something is weighing you down too much, stop what you're doing and give it to God. Pause and make that declaration to Him.

The Promise 💎

When you give those requests to God...

"The peace of God, which transcends all understanding, will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus."

This is the supernatural peace we sang about earlier: "I've got peace that makes no sense." When you're in chaos or a storm of life, you can trust God, release it to Him, and find that transcendental peace.

When the storm comes and you're feeling disoriented, remember this simple declaration:

GOD IS MY ROCK

We need to consistently remind ourselves of God's faithfulness so that we can remain faithful. We need to remind ourselves of the great things God has done in our lives so that we can trust in Him fully. Because we're weak at times, we need these daily reminders constantly to find trust.

Practical Ways to Trust God Deeper ✨

Daily Fresh Start Practices 🌅

These are actionable steps you can implement today to develop deeper trust in God:

🙏

Morning Prayer Routine

Before getting out of bed or checking your phone, acknowledge God and His goodness. Thank Him for the gift of a new day, for His presence, and for His plans.

Try This:

Set your alarm 5 minutes earlier. When it goes off, don't reach for your phone—close your eyes and simply say, "Good morning, God. Thank You for this day. I trust You with everything ahead."

📝

Keep a Prayer Journal

Write down your specific prayer requests so you can track how God answers them. This builds faith as you see His faithfulness over time.

Try This:

Use your phone's notes app or a physical journal. Create three columns: Date | Request | God's Answer. Set a daily notification to remind you to write down what you're praying for.

🙌

Daily Gratitude Practice

Actively record what you're thankful for every single day. Don't take anything for granted—the air in your lungs, food, shelter, relationships, opportunities.

Try This:

Every evening before bed, write down 3-5 specific things you're grateful for that day. Be detailed: instead of "family," write "the way my daughter laughed at dinner tonight."

⏸️

Pause and Declare

Throughout the day when anxiety or worry hits, stop what you're doing and make a declaration of trust to God. Release the burden to Him.

Try This:

When you feel overwhelmed, literally pause. Take three deep breaths and say out loud (or silently): "God is my rock. I will not be shaken." Or simply: "I trust You, God. This is Yours now."

Set 2-3 alarms throughout your day labeled "Pause & Trust" as reminders.

📖

Memorize Trust Scriptures

When storms hit, having Scripture memorized gives you an anchor. Start with short, powerful verses you can recall instantly.

Start With These:

  • "God is my rock. I will not be shaken." (Psalm 62:2)
  • "I believe; help my unbelief." (Mark 9:24)
  • "God is our refuge and strength." (Psalm 46:1)

Write them on sticky notes and place them where you'll see them: bathroom mirror, computer screen, car dashboard.
