üìöPastor Brett's Real-Life Examples

üéØ Example 1: "June" - The Critical Emailer

Situation: A church member who sent critical emails every Monday, always packaged as "suggestions" but consistently negative.

Pastor's Initial Response: Long, detailed, encouraging replies trying to please her.

The Problem: It fed the pattern and made him anxious about preaching, always thinking about potential criticism.

The Boundary: Shorter responses, delayed timing, refusing to let her "live rent-free" in his head.

Lesson: Sometimes you need to change the dynamic to protect your own peace and effectiveness.

üöá Example 2: The Kennedy Town Lunch Pattern

Situation: A friendship where the other person always chose restaurants near his office in Kennedy Town, requiring Pastor Brett to travel.

Pastor's Initial Response: Went along with it but grew increasingly bitter, creating a false narrative about the friend being selfish.

The Boundary: Simply suggested meeting in Causeway Bay instead of always Kennedy Town.

The Surprise: The friend happily agreed, revealing Pastor Brett's assumptions were wrong.

Lesson: Sometimes boundaries protect us from our own false narratives and prevent relationship damage.
