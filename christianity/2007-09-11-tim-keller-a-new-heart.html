II. God's Solution: A Circumcised Heart (v. 6)

Understanding the Heart

In modern culture, we think of the "heart" as the source of emotions and feelings, as opposed to the "head" which is the source of thoughts. But the Bible uses "heart" differently.

Biblical Definition of "Heart":

The heart is the seat of your entire acting self‚Äîcontrolling not just emotions, but also thoughts and actions. It's where your most fundamental commitments reside: the things you most hope in, believe in, look to, and live for.

Whatever you look at and say, "If I had that, then I would be happy, then I would have meaning, then I would have value"‚Äîthat's what shapes everything else in your life.

Understanding Circumcision

Circumcision was the physical ritual representing Israel's commitment to obey God in His covenant. So what does a "circumcised heart" mean?

It means serving God not just because you have to, but because you want to. Not out of external duty alone, but from internal desire and love.

Scripture uses sensory language to describe this transformation. Deuteronomy 29:3-4 speaks of "a heart to understand, eyes to see, and ears to hear." You move from knowing about God to actually seeing, tasting, and hearing Him.

"When I was in college, I took a music appreciation course and I had to listen to a lot of Mozart because it was on the test. I listened to Mozart to get a passing grade in the class, so I could get my degree, so I could get out and get a good job. I listened to Mozart to make money.

People with a new heart love and obey God for the beauty and attractiveness of who He is in Himself‚Äînot because they have to, not to get things, not as a means to an end. They love Him aesthetically, as an end in and of Himself.

People without a new heart obey God to get things‚Äîforgiveness, blessing, heaven. But people with new hearts obey God just to get God, just to be near Him, just to delight Him.

When you receive a new heart, doctrines you've known for years suddenly come alive. Abstractions become realities. You don't just know about God's love‚Äîyou see it. You don't just believe in God's power‚Äîyou taste it.

If you really have a new heart, you get changed. Jonathan Edwards warns against being satisfied with emotional experiences alone:

The new heart produces the fruit of the Spirit in your life: love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control (Galatians 5:22-23). Those around you will notice the difference.

IV. How to Receive a New Heart

‚ñº

The most crucial question: If you don't have a circumcised heart, how do you get one? Keller's answer is profound: Don't just ask God for a new heart. See how God procured it.

The Theology of Circumcision

Why circumcision? Why such a bloody, painful ritual to represent covenant commitment to God? The answer lies in understanding what circumcision symbolized.

üìñ Genesis 15: Abraham's Covenant

When Abraham made his covenant with God, he walked between the torn pieces of a dead animal. This was a visual enactment of the penalty for disobedience.

By walking through the pieces, Abraham was essentially saying: "I promise you my allegiance, and Lord, if I don't do what I'm promising today, may I be torn to pieces."

Circumcision was a reenactment and dramatization of this curse of the covenant.

‚öîÔ∏è The Meaning of "Being Cut Off"

When the Israelites were circumcised, they were saying: "We will obey you, and if we are not obedient to you, may we be cut off."

The punishment for sin‚Äîthe curse of the covenant‚Äîis being cut off:

  • ‚Ä¢ Cut off from other people (isolation)
  • ‚Ä¢ Cut off from your country (exile)
  • ‚Ä¢ Cut off from God (spiritual death)

When you lie, it cuts you off from people. When you cheat or are cruel, it isolates you. The penalty of sin is banishment and exile.

How did God give us a new heart? On the cross, centuries after Deuteronomy 30, on an afternoon that turned into a dark and stormy night:

Jesus was cut:

  • ‚Ä¢ Thorns pressed into His brow

But the sword came down on Jesus. He was cut. He was cut off. So that now we can go in.
